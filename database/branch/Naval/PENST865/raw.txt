Course Objectives: 
1. Understand various test facilities in marine hydrodynamics.  
2. Discern various resistance tests using models and analyse the results 
3. Discern open water tests and self propulsion tests and analyse the results 
4. Understand seakeeping and manoeuvring trials and analyse the results.

Criteria for Evaluation (Evaluate and Analyse): 20 marks 
 
Criteria for evaluation: 
1. Problem Definition (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Clearly defines the experimental setup and the  problem. 
b. Examine and identifies relevant contextual factors (constraints, resources, 
objectives). 
2. Problem Analysis (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Carry out the experiments on models based on Froude’s hypothesis.. 
b. Compare and justify the proposed solutions with technical evidence and 
reasoning. 
3. Evaluate (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Thoroughly evaluate the observed model results for extrapolation to ship’s 
results. 
b. Compares trade-offs, advantages, and disadvantages. 
c. Considers feasibility and practical implications. 
4. Design Implementation (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Identify and select the most accurate result.. 
b. Successfully translates the result into documents. 
5. Conclusion (K4- 2 points, K5 – 2 points) 
a. Summarizes findings and insights. State which solution is most appropriate 
for the problem. (K4) 
b. Reflects critical thinking and informed decision-making. (K5) 
Scoring: 
1. Accomplished (4 points): Exceptional analysis, clear implementation, and depth of 
understanding. 
2. Competent (3 points): Solid performance with minor areas for improvement. 
3. Developing (2 points): Adequate effort but lacks depth or clarity. 
4. Minimal (1 point): Incomplete or significantly flawed.
