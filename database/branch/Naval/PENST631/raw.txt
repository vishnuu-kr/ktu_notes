Course Objectives: 
 
1. Understand various types of shipyard layouts, material handling systems.  
2. Discern various production management methodologies in modern shipbuilding. 
3. Discern the various assembly techniques, welding symbols and standards used in shipbuilding. 
4. Explicate the various types of outfitting and launching methodologies adopted in modern 
shipbuilding.

Module 
No.
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours
1 
General principles for location and layout of shipyards. Functional and 
geographic aspects, transport, supply industry and human resource. Evolution of 
first to fourth generation shipyards and their typical layouts. Product and 
Process Oriented Work Breakdown Structure. Integrated hull construction, 
outfitting and painting (IHOP), grouping and work breakdown structure. Zone 
construction method for hull blocks, outfitting and painting, pipe piece family 
manufacturing. Material handling, storage and transport systems. Material 
preparation. Straightening of plates, sand blasting, painting, lofting, nesting. 
9  
2 
Production management. Pre-production, production and post production stages. 
Planning, design, production, material management. Quality control/ quality 
assurance. Accuracy control. Risk management. Project monitoring – build 
strategy, progress monitoring, PERT charts. Cutting processes and tools 
mechanical cutting, thermal cutting, general description of the various machines, 
photoelectric and NC-control devices. Bending of plates and sections. Heat-line 
bending 
9  

 
 
3 
Assembly of Ship’s Structure. Assembly jigs. Structural arrangement of the fore 
and aft end structure, superstructures, machinery foundation, cranes and davits. 
Welding in shipbuilding, welding methods, standards, symbols. Erection of 
ship’s hull. Handling of pre-assembled units in the erection area,  Different 
methods of hull assembly. Block erection methods – floor, ring and pyramid. 
Single/ multi point erection methods, Technological processes in  the installation 
of main machinery, shafting and propeller. 
9  
4 
Weld defects, distortions and their preventive measures. Testing – destructive/ 
non destructive. Launching- Mechanical Launching Methods (Slip, Lift), 
Construction of slipways for end launch and side launch. Launching curves. 
Arresting arrangements. Tipping, Pivoting/ plunging, drop off. Other Methods of 
launching - Launching by Floating Off (Building Dock, Floating Dock), 
Launching using air bags. 
9
