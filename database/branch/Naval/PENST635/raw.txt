Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the governing flow equations for common flow problems and to understand 
discretisation  of a domain into a mesh 
2. Understand the boundary conditions to be applied. 
3. Understand numerical solution methods and their limitations and to understand the role of 
turbulence and its  influence on fluid flows 
4. Provide the student with a significant level of experience in the use of modern CFD software 
for the analysis of complex fluid-flow systems

Criteria for Evaluation (Evaluate and Analyse): 20 marks 
 
Criteria for evaluation: 
1. Problem Definition (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Clearly defines the CFD problem. 
b. Examine and identify relevant contextual factors (constraints, resources, 
objectives). 
2. Problem Analysis (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Break-down and present a well-reasoned solution approach based on CFD. 
b. Compare and justify the proposed solutions with technical evidence. 
3. Evaluate (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Thoroughly evaluate the proposed solutions for flow problems. 
b. Compare trade-offs, advantages, and disadvantages. 
c. Consider feasibility and practical implications. 

 
 
4. Design Implementation (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Select the most feasible solution by implementing the proposed solutions. 
b. Successfully translate the chosen solution into tangible outputs. 
5. Conclusion (K4- 2 points, K5 – 2 points) 
a. Summarizes findings and insights. State which solution is most appropriate 
for the problem. (K4) 
b. Reflects critical thinking and informed decision-making. (K5) 
Scoring: 
1. Accomplished (4 points): Exceptional analysis, clear implementation, and depth of 
understanding. 
2. Competent (3 points): Solid performance with minor areas for improvement. 
3. Developing (2 points): Adequate effort but lacks depth or clarity. 
4. Minimal (1 point): Incomplete or significantly flawed.
