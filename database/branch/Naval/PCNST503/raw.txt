Course Objectives: 
1. Impart basic knowledge on marine engineering useful for a naval architect and get a broad 
understanding of the layout of Engine Rooms. 
2. Familiarize with various marine machinery used onboard ships.  
3. Familiarize with various systems used onboard ships.  
4. Get an understanding of the shafting system, steering gear and stabilisers.

Marine Piping and major marine Systems: Piping arrangement for 
steam; bilge; ballast and oil fuel systems; lube oil and cooling system 
with various fittings; domestic fresh water and sea water hydrophore 
system. 
Deck Machinery: Deck cranes; windlasses; mooring winches; anchors 
and anchor chains; davits and lifeboats lowering mechanism; cargo 
handling equipment.

Shafting and Propellers: stern tubes and glands, oil lubricated stern 
tubes; shaft seals; shaft alignment; thrust block; propeller types and 
construction details; maintenance and operation of marine propellers. 
Steering Gear: Operation and constructional details of various types of 
steering machinery. 
Ship Stabilizers: Types, operations of different types of passive and
