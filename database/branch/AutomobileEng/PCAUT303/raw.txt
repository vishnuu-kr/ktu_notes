Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the fundamental principles and constructional details of various automotive chassis 
components. 
2. Impart basic knowledge of different types of drivetrains and axles.  
3. Understand the operation of suspension, brakes, wheels, and tyres.

Chassis and Frames: Types of chassis layouts with reference to power 
plant location & drive. Automotive frames: Constructional details & 
various types - four wheeler and two wheeler frames, frame materials, 
loads acting on the frames, defects and remedies. 
Suspension System: Need for suspension system, constructional details 
and characteristics of suspension springs - single leaf, multi Leaf, coil, 
torsion bar, rubber, pneumatic and hydro-elastic. Rigid axle suspension 
system, independent suspension system-types. Shock absorbers-types 
and construction.

2 
Front axle and Steering: Front axles and stub axles, steering geometry 
parameters- significance and effects, conditions for true rolling, steering 
linkages, steering mechanisms, types of steering gear box- recirculating 
ball type, rack and pinion. Steering ratio, reversibility, wheel alignment, 
11 

 
 
power steering system – integral and linkage, power assisted steering. 
 
3 
Drive train: Propeller shaft, constructional details, universal joints & 
constant velocity joints, final drive - types, hotchkiss drive, torque tube 
drive, radius rods, pan hard rods and stabilizers. Differential - working 
principle and constructional details, non-slip differential & differential 
locks. 
Rear axles: Rear axles - live and dead axles, constructional details, 
loads acting on drive axles, rear axle shaft supporting types - semi 
floating, full floating, three quarter floating, axle housings and types, 
multi axle vehicle. 
 
11 
4 
Brakes: Principle, stopping distance & braking efficiency, weight 
transfer during braking, drum brakes. Constructional details, leading 
and trailing shoe, braking torque. Disc brakes, types, constructional 
details, advantages & disadvantages, hydraulic, pneumatic & power-
assisted braking systems, servo brakes, retarders- types. 
Wheels: Construction and function, classification, wheel dimensions. 
Tyres: Structure and function of tyres, classification, static and dynamic 
properties of pneumatic tyres, tyre materials, tyre section & designation, 
factors affecting tyre life, tyre rotation , wheel balancing , quick change 
wheels & special wheels.
