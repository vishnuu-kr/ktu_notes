Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the fundamental properties of various materials used in automotive 
manufacturing, such as metals, polymers, composites, and ceramics.  
2. To understand the latest advancements in automotive materials, including lightweight 
materials, high-strength alloys, and smart materials. 
3. To understand the environmental impact of material selection and manufacturing processes in 
the automotive industry. 
4. To Understand the emerging trends in automotive materials, including electric vehicle 
materials, light weighting for fuel efficiency, and advancements in battery technology

Introduction to Automotive Materials: Overview of material types: metals, 
polymers, composites, and ceramics. Criteria for material selection in the 
automotive industry.  Metals in Automotive Applications; Ferrous and non-
ferrous alloys. Properties Polymers and Composites, Thermoplastics, 
thermosetting plastics, and elastomers. Reinforced plastics, carbon fibre 
composites, and their automotive applications. Applications of steel, 
aluminium, magnesium, and titanium alloys. Ceramics and Glass. Structural 
ceramics, advanced ceramics, and their uses in engines and braking systems. 
Glass materials: properties and applications in automotive glazing.

Applications of advanced high-strength steels (AHSS), aluminium alloys, and 
magnesium alloys. High-Performance Materials, Superalloys, titanium alloys,

Environmental Considerations in Material Selection: Life cycle assessment 
(LCA) of automotive materials. Impact of materials on vehicle emissions and 
fuel efficiency. Strategies for recycling metals, polymers, and composites. 
End-of-life vehicle (ELV) recycling and circular economy concepts. 
Development and application of bio-based and biodegradable materials. Eco-
design principles and their implementation in automotive manufacturing. 
Overview of environmental regulations affecting the automotive industry. 
Standards for automotive material sustainability and compliance.

Materials for Electric Vehicles (EVs): Requirements for light weighting and 
energy efficiency in EVs. Battery materials: lithium-ion, solid-state batteries, 
and future alternatives. Advanced Materials for Fuel Efficiency: 
Aerodynamics, lightweight materials, and their role in reducing fuel 
consumption, Use of composites and advanced alloys in structural 
components.  Smart and Functional Materials: Development of self-healing 
materials, sensors, and conductive materials for automotive applications. 
Integration of smart materials in vehicle systems and components. Future 
Directions in Automotive Materials: Trends in material innovation, 
sustainability, and manufacturing processes. Research and development 
opportunities in new materials and technologies.

Assignment: 20 Marks 
Students should evaluate and analyze a real-world optimization problem, assess the proposed solutions, 
provide a conclusion on which solution is most appropriate for the problem, and implement the chosen 
solution using any specific technique/tool/software/programme.  
 

Criteria for evaluation: 
1. Problem Definition (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Clearly defines the real-world optimization problem. 
b. Examine and identifies relevant contextual factors (constraints, resources, objectives). 
2. Problem Analysis (K4 - 4 points) 
a. Break-down and presents a well-reasoned solution approach. 
b. Compare and justify the proposed solutions with evidence and logical reasoning. 
3. Evaluate (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Thoroughly evaluate the proposed solutions. 
b. Compares trade-offs, advantages, and disadvantages. 
c. Considers feasibility, scalability, and practical implications. 
4. Implementation (K5 - 4 points) 
a. Select the most feasible solution by implementing the proposed solutions/techniques. 
b. Successfully translates the problem to solution using proposed technique. 
c. Demonstrates proficiency in solution practices (readability, efficiency, error handling). 
5. Conclusion (K4- 2 points, K5 – 2 points) 
a. Summarizes findings and insights. State which solution is most appropriate for the problem. 
(K4) 
b. Reflects critical thinking and informed decision-making. (K5) 
Scoring: 
1. Accomplished (4 points): Exceptional analysis, clear implementation, and depth of 
understanding. 
2. Competent (3 points): Solid performance with minor areas for improvement. 
3. Developing (2 points): Adequate effort but lacks depth or clarity. 
4. Minimal (1 point): Incomplete or significantly flawed.
