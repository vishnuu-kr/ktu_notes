Course Objectives: 
 
1. To familiarize the students with various alternative energy sources 
2. To familiarize with energy conversion, utilization and storage for renewable technologies. 
3. To understand the potential of using renewable energy technologies 
4. To understand the environmental aspects of energy usage and conversion

Syllabus Description

Hours 
 
 
1 
Energy Sources: Conventional (fossil fuels, nuclear) and alternative 
(renewable sources like solar, wind, geothermal). Need for alternative 
sources: resource depletion and environmental impact. Non-conventional 
energy sources: solar, wind and geothermal. Classification of alternative 
fuels and drive trains: biofuels, hydrogen, electric, hybrid. Scenario of 
conventional auto fuels & oil reserves: declining availability of conventional 
oil reserves and its impact on fuel prices. 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
2 
Biofuels for SI and CI Engines: Application in spark ignition (SI) and 
compression ignition (CI) engines. Alcohols for SI engines: use of methanol 
and ethanol as SI engine fuels. Manufacturing processes and comparison of 
properties with gasoline. Engine performance with alcohols: impact on 
engine performance and emissions. Alcohol-gasoline blends: gasohol and 
E85, advantages and limitations. Compressed natural gas (CNG), liquefied 
natural gas (LNG) and biogas: availability, properties and engine 
modifications. Biogas: production and application (single or dual fuel). 
Vegetable oils as diesel fuels: straight vegetable oil, biodiesel properties and 
performance in engines. 
 
 
 
 
 
9 

 
 
3 
Hydrogen energy: Properties such as auto ignition temperature, boiling 
temperature, cetane number, flammable limits, flash point, calorific value 
(heating value), latent heat of vaporization, octane number, stoichiometric 
air- fuel ratio, pour point, cloud point, volatility, sulphur, density of 
hydrogen. Sources and Production of Hydrogen: electrolysis of water, 
thermal decomposition and biological processes. Hydrogen storage and 
applications: challenges and methods for storing hydrogen fuel. Applications 
of hydrogen and engine modifications. Hazards and safety systems: safety 
concerns, safety systems required. Performance and emissions, performance 
characteristics of hydrogen engines and emissions. Fuel cells: working 
principle, advantages and limitations. 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
4 
Energy for Electric Vehicles: Methods for collecting and storing solar 
energy, working principle of photovoltaic conversion and electric vehicle 
applications. Wind energy for electric vehicles: introduction to wind 
turbines, types of wind turbines. Electric vehicles - design considerations, 
limitations and opportunities for improvement in electric vehicle technology. 
Electric motors, different types. Batteries-explore various battery types, 
capacities, limitations and future possibilities. Electric vehicle layout & 
hybrid vehicles: advantages, limitations and types of hybrid vehicles.
