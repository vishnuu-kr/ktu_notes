Course Objectives: 
1. To provide students with a comprehensive understanding of supply chain management 
principles and their strategic importance in the automotive industry. 
2. To equip students with the skills to plan, forecast, and optimize supply chain operations, 
focusing on inventory management, production planning, and transportation. 
3. To enable students to apply collaborative strategies and emerging technologies to enhance 
supply chain integration and address challenges in a globalized environment.

1 
Introduction to Supply Chain Management  
Overview of Supply Chain Management (SCM): Definition, importance, and 
key components of SCM in the automotive industry. Supply Chain Concepts 
and Terminology: Understanding the supply chain, logistics, value chain, and 
their interrelations. Automotive Supply Chain Structure: Components of the 
automotive supply chain, including suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, 
retailers, and customers. Supply Chain Models and Frameworks: Overview of 
common supply chain models, such as the SCOR model, and their application 
in the automotive industry. Role of SCM in the Automotive Industry: The 
strategic importance of SCM in reducing costs, improving efficiency, and 
enhancing customer satisfaction in the automotive sector. 
9 
2 
Supply Chain Planning and Strategy  
Strategic Supply Chain Planning: Long-term planning and decision-making in 
supply chain management to align with business goals. Demand Forecasting 
and Planning: Techniques for predicting customer demand and aligning 
9 

production schedules accordingly. Inventory Management: Principles of 
inventory control, optimization strategies, and their importance in the 
automotive supply chain. Production Planning and Control: Integration of 
production schedules with supply chain processes to ensure smooth 
operations. Transportation and Distribution Planning: Planning and 
optimizing the movement of goods from suppliers to customers, focusing on 
efficiency and cost-effectiveness. 
3 
Supply Chain Integration and Collaboration  
Supply Chain Integration: Strategies for achieving seamless integration of 
internal and external supply chain processes. Role of Information Technology 
in SCM: How IT systems and tools like ERP, SCM software, and IoT are used 
to enhance supply chain visibility and efficiency. Collaborative Planning, 
Forecasting, and Replenishment (CPFR): Framework for collaboration 
between supply chain partners to improve demand planning and inventory 
management. Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI): Understanding VMI 
systems and their benefits in reducing inventory levels and improving supplier 
relationships. Case Studies in Supply Chain Collaboration: Analysis of 
successful collaboration practices in the automotive industry and lessons 
learned. 
9 
4 
Emerging Trends and Challenges in Supply Chain Management  
Globalization and Its Impact on Supply Chains: Understanding the effects of 
globalization on supply chain complexity, risk, and management practices. 
Sustainable Supply Chain Management: Strategies for implementing 
environmentally sustainable practices in the automotive supply chain. Risk 
Management in Supply Chains: Identifying and mitigating risks in the supply 
chain, including geopolitical risks, natural disasters, and supply disruptions. 
Agile supply chains, and the move towards a circular economy. Technological 
Innovations in SCM: Exploring the impact of emerging technologies; AI, 
blockchain, and big data analytics on supply chain management.
