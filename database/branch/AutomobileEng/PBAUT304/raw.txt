Course Objectives: 
1. Understand Atomic and Crystalline Structures: Students will learn the characteristics of different 
atomic bonds and crystal structures and how these influence material properties and applications. 
2. Analyse Material Microstructures and Defects: Students will explore various modes of 
deformation, crystallization effects and the role of crystal imperfections in determining the mechanical 
properties of materials. 
3. Apply Principles of Phase Transformations and Alloy Design: Students will gain knowledge of 
phase diagrams, heat treatment processes, alloying principles and the behaviour of advanced materials 
under different conditions.

Fundamentals of Materials: Atomic structure and bonding 
characteristics of covalent, ionic and metallic bonds. Properties based 
on atomic bonding (Briefly), secondary bonds, classification and 
applications. 
Crystallography: Crystal structures: SC, BCC, FCC, HCP (brief 
description). Atomic packing factor (APF), Miller Indices: crystal 
plane and direction. 
Analysis of Microstructure: X-ray diffraction (XRD) with simple 
9 

 
 
problems, Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and Transmission 
electron microscopy (TEM). 
 
Suggestion - Problem Project - Students can choose their focus areas, 
such as investigating different crystal structures, analyzing atomic 
packing factors, or exploring the applications of X-ray diffraction, 
SEM, and TEM in material science.  
2 
Deformation and Crystallization: Deformation and crystallization, 
modes of plastic deformation, slip and twinning, Schmid's law, 
crystallization, effects of grain size, Hall-Petch theory (simple 
problems), crystal imperfections, classification of crystal imperfections 
(point, line, surface). 
Dislocations: Burgers vector, Frank-read source, correlation of 
dislocation density with strength and the concept of nanomaterials, 
grain boundaries, high and low angle boundaries, driving force for grain 
growth, applications, characterization techniques, polishing and 
etching. 
 
Suggestion - Discipline Project- Allowing students to explore specific 
aspects of microstructure and defects, such as slip, twinning, grain 
size effects, or dislocation density, within a defined overall theme. 
9 
3 
Phase Transformations: Constitution of alloys, solid solutions, 
substitutional and interstitial phase diagrams, isomorphous, eutectic, 
eutectoid, peritectic and peritectoid reactions, Iron-carbon equilibrium 
diagram. Classification of steel and cast iron microstructure, properties 
and application.  
Heat Treatment: Time-temperature-transformation (TTT) and 
Continuous cooling transformation (CCT) diagrams with applications, 
tempering, hardenability and Jominy end quench test, surface hardening 
methods.  
9 

 
 
Suggestion - Problem Project - independently explore phase 
transformations, heat treatment processes, and the practical 
applications of TTT and CCT diagrams. 
4 
Alloys: Need for alloying, classification of alloys, Hume-rothery rules. 
Equilibrium diagrams of common binary systems (five types), coring, 
lever rule, and Gibbs' phase rule. Strengthening mechanisms, cold 
working and hot working. 
Fatigue: Mechanism, factors affecting fatigue life, fatigue test, S-N 
curve, endurance limit, methods to improve fatigue life and thermal 
fatigue.  
Advanced Materials: Composite materials, bio-materials, super 
alloys, smart materials and nano materials. 
Suggestion - Problem Project - independently explore alloy 
classifications, strengthening mechanisms, and advanced materials 
such as composites, biomaterials, and nanomaterials.
