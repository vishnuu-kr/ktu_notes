Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the fundamental concepts, pressure measurement techniques and stability of fluids. 
2. Apply fluid mechanics principles to practical applications. 
3. Understand the mechanics of fluid flow through pipes and impact of jet flows to enhance 
knowledge in engineering applications. 
4. Analyse the performance and efficiency of various hydraulic machines.

Fundamental concepts: Properties of fluid - density, specific weight, 
viscosity, surface tension, bulk modulus, compressibility, velocity, rate 
of shear strain, Newton’s law of viscosity, Newtonian and non- 
Newtonian fluids, real and ideal fluids, incompressible and 
compressible fluids. 
Fluid statics: Atmospheric pressure, gauge pressure and absolute 
pressure. Pressure at a point in a fluid, Pascal’s law. Measurement of 
pressure - piezometer, manometers, pressure gauges. Stability of 
bodies, buoyancy, metacenter, center of pressure. 
11 
2 
Fluid flow: Energies in flowing fluid, head - pressure, dynamic, static 
and total head, continuity equation, Eulers equation, Bernoulli’s

equation with assumptions, practical applications.  
Flow rate measurements: Venturi and orifice meters, Notches and 
Weirs (description only). Velocity measurements- Pitot tube and Pitot 
–static tube. 
 
3 
Flow through pipes: Reynolds experiment, Reynolds number, 
Laminar and Turbulent flow, boundary layer formation, developing and 
fully developed flow, Navier-Stokes equation (no derivation), Hagen-
Poiseuille equation, head loss due to friction, friction factor, Mody’s 
chart, Darcy-Weishbach equation, Chezy’s formula, Major and Minor 
losses in pipes 
 
Impact of jets: Stationary and moving vanes – Flat and curved vanes, 
work done and efficiency

Turbines : Impulse and Reaction Turbines – Pelton Wheel –
Constructional features - velocity triangles, speed ratio, jet ratio & work 
done, losses and efficiencies, Francis Turbine – velocity triangles, work 
done and efficiencies. draft tubes – surge tanks – cavitation in turbines 
– governing of turbines – specific speed of turbine. 
 
Pumps: Positive displacement pumps- reciprocating pumps – air 
vessels and their purposes – slip, negative slip and work done and 
 efficiency- indicator diagram- effect of acceleration and friction on 
indicator diagram. Gear pump, vane Pump, lobe pump. Rotodynamic 
pumps- centrifugal pump-impeller, casings –velocity triangle, work 
done, efficiency and losses, priming, cavitation, specific speed. 
Performance characteristics- Multi stage pumps.
