Course Objectives: 
 
1. To help students understand the automotive industry, its trends, challenges, and major players. 
2. Students will learn fundamental marketing concepts and strategies tailored to the 
automotive sector. 
3. By the end of the course, students will develop practical skills in automotive marketing 
management, including product lifecycle management, branding, and sales strategies

Hours 
 
 
 
 
1 
 
Automotive marketing : Overview of automotive industry- understanding 
the structure, evolution and major players in the automotive industry. Market 
trends and challenges - analysing current trends, challenges and opportunities 
shaping the automotive market. Fundamentals of marketing - Introduction to 
marketing concepts and principles with a focus on their application in the 
automotive sector. Consumer behaviour in the automotive sector - 
understanding consumer preferences, decision making processes and buying 
behaviour specific to the automotive market. Market segmentation and 
targeting - techniques for segmenting automotive markets based on 
demographic, psychographic and behavioural factors, strategies for targeting 
specific customer segments. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
9 

 
 
 
2 
 
Automotive product management: Product lifecycle management - 
understanding the stages of the automotive product lifecycle and strategies 
for managing products at each stage. New product development - techniques 
and processes for developing new automotive products, including market 
research, concept development and product testing. Brand management - 
importance of branding in the automotive industry, brand positioning 
strategies and building 
brand equity. Product positioning and differentiation - strategies for 
positioning automotive products in the market and creating points of 
differentiation from competitors. Quality management in automotive sector - 
ensuring quality standards in automotive products and services and their 
impact on marketing strategies. 
 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
 
 
 
3 
 
Automotive 
marketing 
communications: 
Integrated 
marketing 
communications - overview of marketing communication tools and 
channels used in the automotive industry, including advertising, public 
relations, sales promotion and digital marketing. Advertising and media 
planning - planning and executing advertising campaigns for automotive 
products, including media selection, message development and campaign 
evaluation. Digital marketing strategies - leveraging digital channels such as 
social media, search engine marketing and content marketing to reach and 
engage automotive consumers. Customer relationship Management (CRM): 
Importance of CRM in automotive marketing, strategies for building and 
maintaining customer relationships, and leveraging customer data for 
personalized marketing. Brand Activation and Sponsorship: Implementing 
brand activation strategies and leveraging sponsorship opportunities to 
enhance brand visibility and consumer engagement. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
 
 
4 
 
Automotive Retailing: Overview of automotive retailing channels, 
including dealerships, online sales platforms and direct-to-consumer 
models. Sales Management: Techniques for managing automotive sales 
teams, setting sales targets and developing sales strategies. Channel 
Management: Strategies for managing distribution channels in the 
automotive industry, including dealer networks, franchise agreements and 
channel conflict resolution. After-Sales Service and Support: Importance of 
after-sales service in automotive marketing, strategies for enhancing 
customer satisfaction and loyalty through service and support initiatives. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
9 

Legal and Ethical Issues in automotive marketing: Understanding legal and 
ethical considerations in automotive marketing practices, including 
advertising regulations, consumer protection laws, and environmental 
responsibilities.
