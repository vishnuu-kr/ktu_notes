Course Objectives: 
 
1. Understand thermodynamic fundamentals and laws of thermodynamics, including energy 
transformations and system properties. 
2. Apply first and second laws to evaluate cycles, processes, and heat transfer in various systems. 
3. Understand and apply conduction, convection, and radiation principles in designing heat 
exchangers and thermal systems

Second Law of Thermodynamics: Kelvin-Planck and Clausius 
statements and its equivalence - Refrigerators, heat pump–COP – PMM 
II - Reversible and irreversible process Carnot’s theorem - Entropy - 
The Clausius inequality - Availability and irreversibility. 
Pure Substances: Pure substances, phase transformations, triple point, 
properties during change of phase, T-v, p-v and p-T diagram of pure 
substance, p-v-T surface, saturation pressure and temperature, 
T-h and T-s diagrams, h-s diagrams or Mollier charts, steam tables. 
Property calculations using steam tables. 
 
 
 
 
 
11 
 
 
3 
Mixtures of ideal Gases: Mole Fraction, mass fraction, Dalton’s law 
of partial pressure, Amagat’s laws of additive volumes, Gibbs- 
Dalton’s law, equivalent gas constant and molecular weight, properties of 
gas mixtures, internal energy, 
enthalpy, specific heats and entropy, introduction to real gas mixtures- 
Kay’s rule. 
General Thermodynamic Relations: Combined first and second law 
equations – Helmholtz and Gibb’s functions - Maxwell’s relations, Tds 
equations (Derivations not required). The clapeyron equation throttling 
process, Joule thomson coefficient, inversion curve. 
 
 
11 
 
 
 
 
4 
Fundamentals of Heat Transfer: Overview of conduction, convection 
and radiation-Fourier’s law of heat conduction (1 dimensional only). 
Thermal conductivity of materials, basics of convective heat transfer, 
Newton’s law of cooling (1 dimensional only). 
Radiation: Basic concepts of thermal radiation, blackbody radiation 
and emissive power, Stefan-boltzmann law and Planck’s Law. 
Heat Exchangers: Introduction and types of heat exchangers-parallel 
flow and counter-flow heat exchangers. Concept of overall heat transfer 
coefficient using LMTD and NTU methods.
