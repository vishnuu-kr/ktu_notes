Course Objectives: 
1. To enable students to apply techniques on stock markets and trading. 
2. To equip students to identify tools for fundamental and technical analysis, and select derivatives 
and portfolios.

Hours 
1 
Introduction to stock markets: Economic markets, types, stock markets- 
history and evolution of modern stock market, financial securities, types of 
securities, primary markets and secondary markets, trading instruments in 
the market, international stock exchanges, Indian stock exchanges -NSE-
BSE, stock market indices, SEBI and its functions, financial Intermediaries, 
initial public offering (IPO), investment methods in stock markets. 
9 
2 
Trading and trading psychology: Trading process, types of trading 
strategies- scalping intraday method, momentum trading, swing positional 
trading. Risk in trading, types of risks, risk- reward ratio, risk management, 
target and stop loss, hedging in trading. Trading psychology- emotions of 
traders, methods to overcome the emotions, biases of traders, traits of 
successful traders. 
9 
3 
Fundamental and technical analysis: Fundamental analysis- pillars of 
fundamental analysis, top down and bottom up approach, Porter's five forces. 
Financial statements- balance sheet, profit & loss statement, cash flow 
statement. Financial ratios- leverage, liquidity, profitability and solvency 
banking ratios. Valuation of stocks- intrinsic value of stocks. Technical 
9 

 
 
analysis- RWT theory, Dows theory, candlestick patterns, support and 
resistance, bullish, bearish and neutral candlestick patterns, types of technical 
indicators, break out theory. 
4 
Derivatives and portfolios: Options trading - fundamentals, terminologies, 
option Greeks, strategies, option premium calculation problems. Forex 
trading - forex market, currency pairs, transactions. Commodity trading - 
benefits, type of commodities. 
Portfolio management: Ways of portfolio management, steps in portfolio 
management and strategies, portfolio diversification.
