Introduction to biomedical transport phenomena- role of diffusion, 
convection in physiological systems- Fluid kinematics: control volumes, 
velocity field, flow rate, acceleration, stream lines, stream tubes and streak 
lines- conservation relations and boundary conditions. Fluid statics: surface 
tension, membrane and cortical tension -Newtonian and non‐Newtonian 
fluids - Laminar and turbulent flow

2 
Cardiovascular transport system: review of physiological process- Rheology 
of flow of blood: large vessels, small tubes, capillaries- regulation of blood 
flow- Flow in branching vessels-flow in specific arteries- Trans vascular 
transport: Introduction, pathways for trans endothelial transport- Rates of 
trans vascular transport: osmotic pressure- starling’s law of filtration- 
Kedem-Katchalsky equation 
9 
3 
Respiratory transport system: review of physiological process - Oxygen-
haemoglobin dissociation curve – Oxygen levels in blood- The Hill equation 
- Dynamics of oxygenation of blood in lung capillaries - Oxygen and carbon 
dioxide transport in blood oxygenators, bio artificial organs, and tissue 
engineered constructs - Oxygen transport in perfusion bioreactors - Oxygen

Transport in kidneys: review of physiological process- Mechanism of 
transmembrane transport: Direct diffusion, facilitated transport, Active 
transport - Quantitative analysis of Glomerular filtration: Hydraulic 
conductivity of Glomerular Capillaries, Solute transport across Glomerular 
Capillaries - Quantitative analysis of Tubular Reabsorption: Mass balance

osmotic pumps – Controlled release of drugs from transdermal patches –
