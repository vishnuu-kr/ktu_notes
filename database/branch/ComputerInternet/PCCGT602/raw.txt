Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the concepts of Internet of Things and various smart systems.  
2. To describe various IoT communication technologies and hardware components

Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description

Hours 
1 
Overview of Internet of Things: Internet of Things, IoT Conceptual 
framework, IoT Architectural view, Technology behind IoT, Sources of IoT-
IoT development boards, Role of RFID, WSN - M2M communication, 
Examples of IoT, Modified OSI model for IoT/M2M systems. 
8 
2 
Communication Technologies – Wireless Communication Technology, 
Near-Field Communication, RFID, Bluetooth BR/EDR and Bluetooth Low 
Energy, ZigBee IP/ZigBee SE 2.0, Wi-Fi, GPRS/GSM Cellular Networks-
Mobile Internet. Wired Communication - Serial Peripheral Interface, 
UART/USART Serial Communication, I2C 
9 
3 
IoT Hardware: IoT sensors: Analog & Digital sensors, Sensors for measuring 
Temperature, Humidity, Distance, Light, Sound, PIR motion sensor, 
Pressure sensor and Moisture sensor (Working and pin details of all sensors 
should be learnt) IoT actuators: Servo motors, DC motor, Relay. 
9 
4 
Data Acquiring, Organizing, Processing and Analytics: IoT application layer 
terminologies, Data acquiring and storage- Data generation, Data acquisition, 
Data validation, Data Categorization for Storage, Assembly Software for the 
Events, Data store, Data Centre Management, Server management.  
Smart IoT Systems Case study: Smart farming, Home automation, IoT in 
health, Automation in industries, IoT in Vehicles – Connected car, Smart 
grid.
