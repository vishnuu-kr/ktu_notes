Course Objectives: 
1. To establish fundamental principles of Polymer Science in a simple and up-front 
manner and to provide the broad background 
2. Introduces students to basic concepts of polymer microstructures, polymer solutions, 
chemical reactions and properties of polymer materials which acts as the fundamental 
to the core areas of Polymer Engineering course.

Hours 
1 
Introduction: Glass transition temperature - Amorphous state, molecular 
motion, first order and second order transitions, Tg, Tm, secondary 
transitions, free volume, kinetic and thermodynamic views of glass 
transition, factors influencing glass transition temperature, Crystalline state, 
polymorphism, polymer single crystals, lamellae, spherulites, super 
molecular structures, fringed micelle model, degree of crystallinity, factors 
affecting crystallinity, X-ray diffraction 
11 
2 
Polymer solutions- terms and definitions: - types of solutions, Hildebrand 
approach, Flory-Huggin’s theory, thermodynamic view of miscibility, upper 
critical solution temperature (UCST), lower critical solution temperature 
(LCST), concentration regimes in polymer solutions, theta conditions 
11 

 
 
3 
Optical and Electrical Properties of polymers: - Effect of polymer 
structure on dielectric constant, capacitance, dielectric loss, power factor, 
dissipation factor and loss factor, effect of additives on electrical properties 
of polymers, effect of polymer structure on optical properties, clarity, 
transparency, haze, transmittance, reflectance, gloss. 
Chemical reactivity of linear and cross-linked polymers, hydrolysis, 
acidolysis, aminolysis, hydrogenation, addition and substitution reactions, 
cross linking reactions, reactivity of functional groups. 
11 
4 
Polymer analogous reactions: - polymer bound reagents, chain end and 
random degradation, degradation by oxygen, ozone, heat, UV light, 
microorganism, crazing, weathering, stabilisation to prevent degradation.
