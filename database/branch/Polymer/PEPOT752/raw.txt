SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Characteristics and selection of separation process: Importance and variety of 
separation, economic significance, inherent separation factor, selection, factors 
influencing the choice of separation process, solvent selection, selection of 
equipment. Recent advances in separation techniques based on size, surface 
properties, ionic properties and other special characteristics of substances, Rate 
based versus equilibrium separation processes, Selection of separation process, 
Energy requirements of separation processes. Polymer materials used as 
membranes, functional polymer membranes 
9  
2 
Introduction, Type and choice of membranes, Plate and frame, tubular, spiral 
wound and hollow fibre membrane reactors and their relative merits, 
Membrane filtration, Microfiltration, Nanofiltration, Ultrafiltration, Reverse 
Osmosis, Dialysis, Models for membrane separations. 
9  

 
 
Basic principles, classifications, Surfactants at Inter phases and in bulk, Foam 
fractionation, Foam flotation, Adsorptive bubble separations, Ion flotation, 
Microemulsion/Macro emulsions, Hydro topes, Solvent ablation 
3 
Interacting solutes, Adiabatic adsorbers, velocity effects. Adsorption- 
Desorption operations: Thermal desorption of gases, activated carbon solute 
recovery, processing liquid using thermal regeneration, Pressure swing and 
vacuum swing adsorption, Regeneration with purge and desorbent. Basics of 
Ionex change, Ionex change resins, Binary ion exchange equilibrium, Ion 
movement theory, Applications, Applications without exchange: Ion 
exclusion, Mass transfer in ion exchange systems 
9  
4 
Introduction, types of chromatography, Elution chromatography: Principles 
and Retention theory, Band broadening and separation efficiency, Types of 
chromatography, large scale elution (cyclic/batch) chromatography, Selective 
adsorption of biological macromolecules, Simulated counter current 
techniques, Comparison with other separation methods. 
9

MODERN METHODS OF INSTRUMENTATION

Course Type 
Theory 
 
Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the principles and applications of modern instrumentation methods in 
polymer engineering. 
2. Gain knowledge about different characterization and testing techniques specific to 
polymers. 
3. Learn advanced measurement techniques and data acquisition methods. 
4. Explore emerging technologies and their applications in polymer instrumentation. 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Fundamentals of Instrumentation in Polymer Engineering 
Introduction to Instrumentation in Polymer Engineering: Importance 
and applications of instrumentation in polymer engineering. Overview 
of traditional vs. modern instrumentation methods. Measurement 
Systems: Elements of a measurement system. Static and dynamic 
characteristics of instruments. Sensors and Transducers Specific to 
Polymers: Principles of sensors and transducers. Types of sensors: 
temperature, pressure, strain, and humidity sensors used in polymer 
processing. Signal Conditioning and Data Acquisition: Amplifiers, 
filters, and converters. Analog to Digital and Digital to Analog 
9  

 
 
Conversion (ADC/DAC). Components of Data Acquisition Systems 
(DAS) and their applications in polymer engineering.  
2 
Characterization Techniques for Polymers 
Thermal 
Analysis: 
Differential 
Scanning 
Calorimetry 
(DSC). 
Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA). Dynamic Mechanical Analysis (DMA). 
Spectroscopic Methods: Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR). 
Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) Spectroscopy. Raman Spectroscopy. 
Chromatographic Techniques: Gel Permeation Chromatography (GPC). High-
Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC). Microscopy and Imaging: 
Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM). Transmission electron microscopy 
(TEM). Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM). 
9  
3 
Advanced Instrumentation in Polymer Processing and Testing 
Process Instrumentation: Instrumentation for extrusion, injection molding, and 
blow molding. Real-time monitoring and control in polymer processing. 
Mechanical Testing of Polymers: Tensile, compressive, and flexural testing. 
Impact and hardness testing. Rheological Measurements: Principles of 
rheology and viscoelastic measurements. Rheometers and their applications in 
polymer engineering. Surface and Interface Characterization: Contact angle 
measurements. Surface energy analysis. Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) for 
surface characterization. 
9  
4 
Emerging Technologies and Applications in Polymer Instrumentation 
Smart Sensors and IoT in Polymer Engineering: Integration of IoT in polymer 
instrumentation. Smart sensors and real-time data analytics. Polymer 
Nanocomposites and Instrumentation: Characterization techniques for 
nanocomposites. Challenges and solutions in nano composite instrumentation. 
Biomedical Applications of Polymers: Instrumentation in the development of 
polymer-based biomedical devices Characterization of biocompatible 
polymers. Sustainability and Environmental Monitoring: Instrumentation for 
recycling and degradation of polymers. Monitoring environmental impact of 
polymer materials. 
9
