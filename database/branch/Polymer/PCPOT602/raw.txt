Course Objectives: 
1. To provide in depth knowledge about focuses on the testing, analysis and characterization of 
polymeric materials. 
2. Student will be able to understand the international testing standards. 
3. Student will be able to perform testing of polymer products as per technically specified standards.

Hours 
1 
Introduction – Need for testing, need for standards and specifications, national 
and international standards BIS, ASTM, BS, DIN, FDA etc. Specimen 
preparation and conditioning, standard for test specimen preparation like 
moulding, machining, stamping and punching of specimens, conditioning of 
specimen. 
9  
2 
Mechanical properties: a) Short term strengths: Tensile properties, compression 
properties, flexural properties, shear properties, impact resistance, toughness, 
Tear resistance, abrasion resistance and hardness. b) Long term strengths: 
Dynamic stress and strain properties and their measurements, creep, stress 
relaxation, fatigue properties, flexing, and resilience, heat build- up, ageing 
properties. 
9  

 
 
3 
Structural evaluation of polymers: Fourier transform infrared spectrometry, 
Ultraviolet - visible spectrometry, Nuclear magnetic resonance spectrometry, 
Mass spectrometry, X-ray diffraction spectrometry, Gas chromatography, 
Scanning Electron 
Microscopy, atomic force microscopy. Electrical properties: volume resistivity, 
surface resistivity, breaks down voltage, dielectric strength, dielectric constant, 
arc resistance, power factor. 
9  
4 
Thermal properties of polymers: Glass transition temperature, melting 
temperature, heat distortion temperature, etc.  Principle and uses of DTA, TGA, 
DSC and TMA. Optical properties: Gloss, haze, refractive index, transmittance. 
Other test: MFI, MVI, specific gravity, weathering properties, toxicity, resistance 
to chemicals, VST, HDT.
