Course Objectives: 
1. 
To understand the structure property relationship in rubbers 
2. 
 To know about the properties and application of general purpose and special purpose   rubbers 
3. 
To understand the role of compounding ingredients and mechanism of vulcanization in rubbers 
4. 
To get familiarized with rubber processing techniques and equipment

Hours 
1 
Natural rubber: - Natural rubber, source, chemical formula, molecular 
weight distribution, crystallisation, structure-property relationship, chemical 
reactivity, electrical and oxidation properties, Various forms of natural rubber- 
crumb, sheet, crepe, SP rubber, DPNR, LNR, liquid rubber, powdered rubber 
11 
2 
Vulcanisation:- Chemical and physical aspects, curing characteristics such 
as scorch time, induction time, cure time.  Mechanism of crosslinking by 
different crosslinking agents- sulphur, sulphur monochloride, phenolic 
resins, metal oxide, diamines, peroxides, oximes. Different curing systems, 
EV, semi-EV, conventional and sulphur less cure, Vulcanisation techniques. 
11 
3 
Classification of elastomers: -Manufacture, structure, properties, processing, 
vulcanisation and applications of SBR, polybutadiene and polyisoprene 
rubber, IIR, NBR, CR, PU, and silicone rubbers.  
11 

 
 
4 
Elastomers: - Monomer preparation, polymerization, structure, properties and 
application of the following rubbers- chlorosulphonated Rubbers, fluorine 
containing rubbers, acrylic rubbers, polysulphide rubber, polyalkenamers, 
polynorbornene, thermoplastic elastomers.  
11
