Course Objectives: 
1. This course aims to impart the basic concepts of continuous and discrete signals and systems. 
2. This course aims to develop understanding about frequency domain approaches used for 
analysis of continuous and discrete time signals and systems. 
3. This course introduces various schemes of analog and digital modulation used in 
communication systems.

Course Type

Course Objectives: 
1. Identify and describe the characteristics and operations of various power semiconductor 
devices. 
2. Comprehend the operation and design of basic power converter circuits. 
3. Implement control strategies in power electronic converters. 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Power semiconductor devices:  
Power diodes, power transistors, thyristor family, SCRs, Triac, GTOs, 
power MOSFETs, IGBTs, MCTs-static and dynamic characteristics, 
protection circuits, series and parallel connections, turn-on characteristics, 
turn off characteristics. 
 
9 
2 
DC-DC Converters  
Step down and Step up choppers – Single-quadrant and Two-quadrant 
chopper – control schemes Switching regulators – Buck, Boost & Buck-
boost –Operation with continuous conduction mode – Waveforms 
 
9 
3 
DC-AC Converters (Inverters)  
Basic principles of inverters and types (square wave, sine wave, PWM 
inverters). Performance parameters and harmonic analysis. Applications in 
UPS, motor drives, and grid-tied renewable energy systems 
AC-DC Converters (Rectifiers) 
Operation of half-wave and full-wave rectifiers. Bridge rectifiers and 
performance parameters (ripple, efficiency). 
9 

 
 
4 
Control Techniques in Power Electronics  
Pulse Width Modulation (PWM): Introduction to PWM techniques, Types 
of PWM (single-pulse, multiple-pulse, sinusoidal PWM). Applications in 
voltage regulation and motor control.  
Industrial Applications and Practical Considerations 
Power Electronics in Renewable Energy, Role of power electronics in solar 
PV and wind energy systems. Role of power electronics in motor control. 
Variable frequency drives (VFD). 
 
9
