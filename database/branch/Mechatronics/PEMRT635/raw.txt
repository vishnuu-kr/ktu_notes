Course Objectives: 
1. To introduce students to the fundamental concepts and techniques of Soft Computing, 
including Neural Networks, Fuzzy Logic, and Genetic Algorithms. 
2. To enable students to apply Soft Computing methods to real-world problems through project-
based learning.

Overview of Soft Computing
Definition and importance of Soft Computing - Comparison with Hard 
Computing - Applications in various domains. 
Introduction to Neural Networks 
Biological inspiration and McCulloch-Pitts model - Activation functions: 
Types and uses - Neural Network Architectures: Single layer feedforward 
networks - Multilayer feedforward networks - Recurrent networks. 
Learning Process in Neural Networks 
Supervised vs. Unsupervised learning - Learning rules: Perceptron, Widrow-
Hoff, Delta rule, Hebbian and Competitive learning - Applications: Pattern 
association, recognition, and function approximation.

Single Layer Networks
Perceptron and ADALINE - Adaptive filtering and optimisation techniques - 
Perceptron convergence theorem.

Multilayer Networks
Architecture and learning algorithms - Backpropagation: Detailed working, 
issues, and solutions - XOR problem and decision rules - Introduction to 
MADALINE. 
Radial Basis Neural Networks 
Concept and applications - Network training and regularisation theory - 
Comparison with Multilayer Perceptron. 
3 
Basics of Fuzzy Logic
Introduction to Fuzzy Sets - Properties, operations, and relations - 
Membership functions and fuzzy rules. 
Fuzzy Rule-Based Systems 
Canonical rule forms - Composite rules and their decomposition - 
Aggregation of fuzzy rules and decision-making. 
Introduction to Genetic Algorithms 
Evolutionary concepts and GA features - Coding, selection, crossover, and 
mutation - Basic GA structure and operators.

Hybrid Systems
Concept of Neuro-Fuzzy Systems - Integration of neural networks and fuzzy 
logic - Introduction to Neuro-Genetic Systems. 
Advanced Topics and Cross-Validation
