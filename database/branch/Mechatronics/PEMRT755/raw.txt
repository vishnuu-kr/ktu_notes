Course Objectives: 
1. Develop a comprehensive understanding of digital image processing techniques, including 
image enhancement, restoration, and compression, to effectively analyze and manipulate 
visual data. 
 
2. Gain practical skills in implementing and evaluating various image segmentation methods, 
ensuring proficiency in both spatial and frequency domain approaches for diverse 
applications.

Digital Image Fundamentals: 
Steps in Digital Image Processing – Components – Elements of Visual 
Perception – simple image formation model. Relationships between pixels – 
Colour image fundamentals – RGB, HSI models, Review of matrix theory: 
row and column ordering- Toeplitz, Circulant matrices. 
9 
2 
Image Enhancement: 
Spatial domain methods: point processing- intensity transformations, 
histogram processing. Spatial filtering- smoothing filters, sharpening 
filters. 
Frequency domain methods: low pass filtering, high pass filtering, 
homomorphic filter 
9 
3 
Image Restoration:  
9 

Degradation model, Unconstraint restoration and constraint restoration.  
Inverse filtering, Weiner filtering. 
Image Compression:  
Need for compression, Basics of lossless compression and lossy 
compression. Concept of transform coding, JPEG Image compression 
standard. 
4 
Image segmentation: Classification of Image segmentation techniques, 
Region approach - Region Growing, Region Split & Merge. Segmentation 
based on thresholding. Edge based segmentation-edge detection, Hough 
transform.
