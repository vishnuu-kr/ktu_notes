Course Objectives: 
1. The course aims to give a brief introduction to human physiology. 
2. The course presents various instrumentation systems used for measurement 
and analysis of physiological parameters.

1 
Introduction to Biomedical Instrumentation Overview of biomedical 
instrumentation – Basic principles and concepts –Source of bioelectric 
potentials - Resting and action potential – Propagation of action potentials 
- Bioelectric potentials example (ECG, EEG, EMG) - Electrode skin 
interface - Types of electrodes - Limb electrodes – Floating electrodes - 
Pre-gelled disposable electrodes - Micro, needle and surface electrodes. 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
2 
Physiological measurements Heart and cardio vascular system - Electro 
conduction system of the heart-Electrocardiograph(ECG) – Electrodes and 
leads – Einthoven triangle - ECG read out devices - ECG machine – Block 
diagram - Measurement of blood flow and cardiac output - Measurement 
of heart sounds – Phonocardiography - Electroencephalogram (EEG) 
measurement - Electromyogram (EMG). 
 
 
 
 
9 
 
3 
Biomedical Imaging Techniques X-rays - Principles of generation - Uses 
of X-rays - Diagnostic still picture – Fluoroscopy – Angiography – 
Endoscopy - Diathermy - Basic principle of computed tomography 
 

(CT) - Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) system and nuclear medicine
imaging system (PET, SPECT) – Radiation therapy - Ultrasound
imaging system - Introduction and basic principle 
 
 
 
4 
Electrical safety and Therapeutic Devices Physiological effects of 
electric current – Shock hazards from electrical equipment, Leakage 
current - Electrical safety analyser – Testing of biomedical equipment – 
Method of accident prevention. Cardiac pacemakers – Internal and 
external pacemakers – Defibrillators –Artificial heart valves - Heart lung 
machine - Biotelemetry - Introduction to telemedicine - Cyber medicine.
