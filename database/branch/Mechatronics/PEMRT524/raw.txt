Course Objectives: 
1. To provide students with a solid foundation in numerical methods for solving engineering 
problems using Python. 
2. To enable students to implement numerical techniques for nonlinear equations, interpolation, 
integration, differentiation, and ordinary differential equations. 
3. To develop students' skills in applying numerical methods to analyse and interpret 
engineering data and solve real-world problems.

Bisection method; Newton-Raphson method; Secant method; False

Interpolation (9 Hours) (Derivation or proof not required) 
Finite difference interpolation: Newton's finite and backward difference 
interpolation methods. 
Polynomial Interpolation: Lagrange interpolation and Newton’s divided 
difference interpolation. 
Spline Interpolation: Cubic splines and B-splines. 
Error analysis in interpolation.

Numerical Integration and Differentiation (6 Hours) 
Numerical Differentiation: Finite difference methods; Error analysis in 
numerical differentiation. 
Numerical Integration: Trapezoidal rule, Simpson’s rule, Gaussian 
quadrature, Adaptive quadrature methods. 
Writing Python programs to perform numerical differentiation and

Solution of Ordinary Differential Equations (7 Hours) 
Initial Value Problems: Euler’s method; Runge-Kutta methods; Multistep 
methods (Adams-Bashforth and Adams-Moulton methods). 
Boundary Value Problems: Shooting method; Finite difference method.
