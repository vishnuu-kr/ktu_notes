Course Objectives: 
1. This course will give an understanding of the light -tissue interactions and the different 
applications of lasers in imaging, diagnosis, and therapy.

Hours 
1 
Bio photonics and Tissue Optics: Overview, Light transport in Tissue-
Radiation transport theory, radiation transport model-basic parameters, 
scattering phase function, radiation transport equation, Tissue properties-
refractive indices, scattering properties, absorption properties. Optothermal 
interactions-temperature rise and tissue damage, optothermal and opto-
acoustic effects. Lasers for bio photonics: Basic laser action, laser diodes, 
CO2 lasers, Nd-YAG laser, optical fiber lasers.  
9

Bio 
imaging: 
Near 
field 
optical 
microscopy-basic 
principles, 
instrumentation-optical probes, biological applications-investigation of cell 
material, Optical Coherence tomography imaging-principles of operation, 
image generation in OCT, OCT system, Ultrahigh-Resolution Optical 
Coherence Tomography. Hologram and Medical Applications: Types of 
Hologram, Advantages & Disadvantages. 
 9 
3 
Lasers in Diagnosis: Bio photonics for blood analysis, optical inference 
and control of blood physiology – glucose sensing, haemoglobin 
oxygenation, tissue oxygen analysis- fundamentals, clinical applications of 
NIR oxygenation monitoring, early optical diagnosis of pressure ulcers- 
clinical diagnostics – evaluation of tissue blood perfusion, local tissue 
metabolism and oxygen supply, local tissue structure atrophy.  
9 

 
 
4 
Lasers in Treatment: Laser tissue welding-principles, benefits and 
limitations, mechanism of laser tissue welding-photochemical and photo 
thermal welding, therapeutic application of lasers in gastroenterology- 
lasers for destruction of tumors, lasers for lithotripsy, Lasers in restorative 
dentistry, laser ablation of hard tissues.
