Course Objectives: 
1. Students will learn various ECG data compression techniques, including direct data 
compression and transformation compression, as well as other techniques. They will also 
be able to apply ECG signal processing methods 
2. Students will gain knowledge about the fundamentals of speech signal processing and 
analyze speech signals through short-time speech analysis, frequency domain analysis, 
and understand key measures. 
3. Students will understand the modeling of EEG signals, and will learn methods for 
reducing artefacts in EEG signals. They will be able to conduct both parametric and non-
parametric spectral analysis, and perform EEG segmentation.  
4. For EMG signal processing, students will explore applications in various areas, as well as 
techniques for amplitude estimation, spectral analysis, and conduction velocity estimation 
of surface EMG signals.

Criteria for evaluation: 
1. Problem Definition (K4 - 5 points) 
a. Clearly defines the problem. 
b. Examines and identifies the relevant factors. 
2. Problem Analysis (K4 - 5 points) 
a. Break-down and presents a well-reasoned solution approach. 
3. Evaluate (K5 - 5 points) 
a. Thoroughly evaluate the proposed solutions. 
4. Conclusion (K3- 3 points, K5 – 2 points) 
a. Summarizes findings and insights. State which solution is most appropriate for the 
problem 
Scoring: 
1. Accomplished (4 points): Exceptional analysis, and depth of understanding. 
2. Competent (3 points): Solid performance with minor areas for improvement. 
3. Developing (2 points): Adequate effort but lacks depth or clarity. 
Minimal (1 point): Incomplete or significantly flawed.
