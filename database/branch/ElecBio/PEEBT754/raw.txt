Course Objectives: 
1. This course aims to introduce students to the foundational concepts and technologies of 
biofabrication, including the principles of tissue engineering, biomaterials, and various 
fabrication techniques. Students will develop a deep understanding of scaffold design, 
cell culture methods, and advanced biofabrication technologies, preparing them to 
innovate in the field of regenerative medicine.  
2. This course focuses on the practical applications of tissue engineering in developing 
advanced tissue substitutes and regenerative solutions. Students will explore specific 
applications such as skin, bone, and cartilage engineering, and learn to utilize cutting-
edge technologies including 3D bioprinting and nanocomposite scaffolds, equipping them 
with skills to tackle real-world challenges in tissue engineering.
