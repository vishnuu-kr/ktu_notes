Course Objectives: 
 
1. Learn the basic principles of biosensors, transducers & their applications. 
2. Provide students an insight into biopotentials and electrodes. 
3. Introduce Nano biosensors & their applications.

Transducers: Classification of transducers, characteristic of transducers. 
Temperature transducers: Resistance temperature detector (RTD), Thermistor, 
Thermocouple, 
chemical 
thermometry. 
Displacement 
transducers: 
potentiometer, resistive strain gauges, inductive displacement, capacitive 
displacement transducer. Pressure transducer: variable capacitance pressure 
transducers, LVDT transducers, strain gauge transducers, catheter tip transducers, 
Piezoelectric transducer. Photoelectric transducers: photo-emissive tubes, 
photovoltaic cell, photoconductive cell, photodiodes. 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2 
Bio-Potential Electrodes: Electrode theory, Skin Contact Impedance. Types of 
Electrodes: Microelectrodes-Metal microelectrodes, micropipette (metal filled 
glass and glass micropipette electrodes). Depth and needle electrodes- Depth 
electrode, Needle electrode. Surface electrodes- Metal plate surface electrodes, 
Suction Cup electrode, Adhesive tape electrode, Multipoint electrode. 
Bio-potentials: ECG - Generation of cardiac action potentials- Characteristics of 
ECG Signal, Einthoven’s triangle -Lead Systems. Electrodes for ECG 
acquisition. EEG – Electroencephalogram - Brain action potentials - 
Characteristics of signal-Electrodes and 10-20 system for EEG signal acquisition. 
Evoked Potentials. EMG - Electrical activity of muscles – Characteristics of 
EMG signal. Electrodes for EMG acquisition. 
(Instrumentation not needed for ECG, EEG & EMG) 
 
 
 
 
 
 
9 

 
 
 
 
3 
Fundamentals of Biosensors-Basic Principle, Characteristics of a Biosensor. 
Types - Based on Transducer: Electrochemical- Amperometric, Potentiometric; 
optical-Surface plasmon resonance, Evanescent Wave Fluorescence Biosensors; 
piezo electric; calorimetric biosensors. Based on Bio recognition elements in 
biosensors- natural-semisynthetic-synthetic. immobilization methods ISFET, 
Enzyme electrodes. Principles of Fiber-Optic Sensing- Definitions, 
Classification, Working Principles. Applications of biosensors-Biosensors for 
clinical diagnostics-Glucose detection- cancer detection. 
 
 
 
9 
 
 
 
4 
Nano biosensors: Significance, Types of Nanomaterials- Gold Nano- particles, 
Quantum Dots, Carbon Nanotubes & Graphene (Key features). Synthesis of 
Nanomaterials-Top-down Approach & Bottom down Approach. Types of nano 
biosensors: nanotubes-based sensors, nanoparticles-based biosensors, nanowire-
based sensors, quantum dots-based sensors (Basics of sensing only). Application 
of Nanomaterial based biosensors: Metal nanoparticle-based biosensors, carbon 
nanotube-based biosensors.
