Course Objectives: 
1. Comprehend the various challenges involved in the design and implementation of upper limb 
prostheses, including anatomical, functional, and technical constraints. 
2. Investigate different types of upper limb prostheses and evaluate their effectiveness, 
considering the needs of diverse user groups and the limitations imposed by current 
technologies. 
3. Utilize knowledge of the human motor system and control strategies to innovate and improve 
the development of upper limb prosthetics, ensuring better integration with the user's natural 
movements and enhancing overall usability.
