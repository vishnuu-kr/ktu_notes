Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the structure and function of excitable cells, explore the ionic basis of cardiac 
action potentials and the specialized conductive system of the heart. 
2. Learn to generate, interpret, and analyze ECG waveforms, as well as understand EEG brain 
rhythms, signal characteristics, and electrode systems. Study EMG signals, along with the 
characteristics of heart sounds. 
3. Identify and mitigate various types of noise in biopotential recordings using time domain 
filters and frequency domain filters. Perform power spectrum analysis and conduct spectrum 
analysis of bio signals.
