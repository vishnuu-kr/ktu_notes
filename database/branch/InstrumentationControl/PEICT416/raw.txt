Course Objectives: 
 
1. To familiarize with the devices for measuring displacement, vibration, force and torque 
2. To familiarize with the devices for angular and linear measurements 
3. To understand the concept of fluid mechanics and its applications

Course Objectives:

2. Design and Simulation Skills for Combinational and Sequential Circuits 
3. Advanced Digital Design and FPGA Implementation 
 
SYLLABUS 
 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
 
1 
Introduction to Verilog: Overview of HDL-Importance and applications of 
Verilog-Basic syntax and structure of a Verilog Program-Data types and 
operators 
Combinational Logic Design: Design and simulation of combinational 
circuits using Verilog-Examples: Half Adder, Full Adder, Multiplexer, 
Demultiplexer 
Sequential Logic Design: Design and simulation of sequential circuits using 
Verilog-D Flip-Flop, T Flip-Flop, JK Flip-Flop, 4-bit Counter 
 
9

Verilog Programming Constructs: Continuous assignments - Procedural 
assignments (always blocks) - Blocking and non-blocking assignments - Case 
statements and if-else constructs 
Modules and Hierarchical Design: Modules and ports-Module instantiation-
Hierarchical design concepts-Examples: Designing a simple ALU, 4-bit 
Adder/Subtractor 
Testbenches and Simulation: Writing testbenches for Verilog designs - 
Introduction to simulation tools -Timing and delays in Verilog - Simulation 
examples and debugging 
 
9

asynchronous circuits-synthesis of asynchronous circuits- Race condition- 
State reduction – State assignment – Transition diagram
