Course Objectives: 
 
1. Identify and describe the characteristics and operations of various power 
semiconductor devices. 
2. Comprehend the operation and design of basic power converter circuits. 
3. Implement control strategies in power electronic converters.

AC-DC Converters (Rectifiers) 
Operation of half-wave and full-wave rectifiers. 
Bridge rectifiers and performance parameters (ripple, efficiency). 
DC-DC Converters 
Types of DC-DC converters (buck, boost, buck-boost, Cuk). 
Mode of operation, duty cycle calculations, and efficiency analysis. Design 
and application examples in power supplies and renewable energy systems. 
DC-AC Converters (Inverters) 
Basic principles of inverters and types (square wave, sine wave, PWM
inverters).

Performance parameters and harmonic analysis.

Control Techniques in Power Electronics -Pulse Width Modulation
(PWM) 
Introduction to PWM techniques. 
Types of PWM (single-pulse, multiple-pulse, sinusoidal 
PWM). Applications in voltage regulation and motor control. 
Feedback Control Systems 
Basics of feedback control systems in power electronics. Implementation of
feedback loops in power converters 
 
6 
 
 
 
 
 
 
4 
Industrial Applications and Practical Considerations Power Electronics 
in Renewable Energy 
Role of power electronics in solar PV and wind energy systems. Maximum 
Power Point Tracking (MPPT) techniques. 
Grid integration and storage solutions. 
Motor Drives and Control 
Types of motors used in industrial applications (DC, induction,
synchronous). 
Role of power electronics in motor control. 
Variable frequency drives (VFD) and their design considerations. 
Practical Issues and Emerging Trends 
Thermal management, EMI/EMC, and protection strategies in power
electronics. 
Reliability and safety standards. 
Emerging technologies and future trends in power electronics (wide bandgap
semiconductors, smart grids, IoT integration).
