Introduction to Environmental Engineering 
An overview of environmental laws and regulations. Necessity of 
Instrumentation & Control for environment, sensor requirement for 
environment, Classification of pollution: Air pollution, water pollution, soil 
pollution, marine pollution, noise pollution, thermal pollution, nuclear 
hazards and hazardous waste, occupational safety and health. Causes, 
Effects and control of all types of Pollution. 
 
9 
2 
Quality of water 
Standards of raw & treated water, sources of water & their natural quality, 
effects of water quality. Water quality parameters: Thermal conductivity, 
dissolved oxygen, anions, turbidity. Water Quality detectors, Opacity 
monitors, conductivity analysers & their application. Spectrometric 
methods- emission spectrograph- atomic absorption spectra. 
 
9 
3 
Air monitoring 
Measurement of ambient air quality. Air pollution control of stationary 
sources: gaseous pollutants and particulate pollutants. Air pollution control 
of mobile sources: automobile emissions. Noise pollution: effect of noise 
pollution on people and community, noise area classification and hearing 
conservation. Noise sources and criteria, noise level measurement, noise 
absorption techniques and noise control. 
9 

 
 
4 
Pollution control in industries 
pollution control in petroleum refineries, fertilizer industries, pulp and 
paper industries, cement industries. Pollution case studies - disaster 
management: floods, earthquake, cyclone and landslides, Environmental 
disaster monitoring. 
Handling and management of hospital wastes.
