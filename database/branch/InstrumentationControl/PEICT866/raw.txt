Course Objectives: 
1. It aims to explain the concept of corrosion and different types of corrosion, and also helps the 
students to select a suitable corrosion control or prevention technique.

Hours 
1 
Introduction to Corrosion 
Historical background, definitions, corrosive environment, consequences of 
corrosion, cost of corrosion, breakdown of spending on corrosion, corrosion 
science and corrosion engineering, functional aspects of corrosion. 
Basic Concepts in Corrosion 
 
Anodic and cathodic reaction, Anodic reactions characteristics, Cathodic 
reactions characteristics, types of corrosion cells, mechanism of corrosion of 
Iron, concept of free energy, reversible electrode potential, concentration 
cell, liquid junction potential, application of free energy to corrosion cells, 
Nernst equation, sign convention, reference electrodes 
 
9 
2 
Corrosion Kinetics and Measurement 
Faraday’s Laws, Corrosion Kinetics, Helmholtz double layer, reverse 
reaction (cathodic reaction), departure from equilibrium, measurement of 
corrosion, determination of corrosion rates by electrochemical 
measurements, polarisation linearization, kinetics of passivity, definition of 
important electrochemical parameters for active passive metals, control of 
passivity, effect of environment. 
 
9 

 
 
3 
Cathodic Protection 
Basis of cathodic protection, working of cathodic protection system, factors 
leading to corrosion of underground metallic structures, electrical basis of 
cathodic protection, electrochemical theory of cathodic protection, anodic 
polarisation, cathodic protection system, components of Galvanic systems, 
impressed current system, protection of submarine pipelines, design 
parameters in cathode protection, measurements in cathodic protection. 
9 
4 
Corrosion Control by Inhibition 
Scope of inhibitor, examples of application of inhibitors, important 
consideration in selection of inhibitors, important terms related to inhibitors, 
classification of inhibitors, description of inhibitors, inorganic anodic 
inhibitors, organic inhibitors, synergistic inhibitors, scaven gers, neutralisers, 
scale inhibitors, rebar inhibitors, inhibitor application techniques, inhibitor 
efficiency and inhibitor concentration 
 
9
