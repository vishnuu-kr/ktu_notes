Course Objectives: 
1. Explore the history of aviation and space flight, including airplane and space vehicle 
anatomy, aerodynamics, flight performance, and rocket propulsion principles. 
2. Understand the operation and application of various flight and navigation instruments, 
including GPS, GNSS, automatic pilots, and advanced inertial sensors used in guidance and 
avionics systems.

Course Objectives: 
1. Gain a solid understanding of the fundamental principles behind modern analytical 
instruments. 
 
2. Learn the theoretical foundations and practical applications of various modern analytical 
techniques used in chemical and biochemical analysis. 
3. Learn how to select appropriate instruments based on analytical needs. 
4. Develop skills in data acquisition, interpretation, and analysis using modern software and 
computational tools.
