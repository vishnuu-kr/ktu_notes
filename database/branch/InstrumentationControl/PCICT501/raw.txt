Course Objectives: 
1. To introduce students to the foundational concepts of control systems, including the 
differences between open-loop and closed-loop systems and the historical development of 
automatic control. 
2. To develop the ability to analyse control systems' time-domain and frequency-domain 
behaviours, focusing on the response characteristics and stability criteria. 
3. To equip students with the tools and techniques for modelling, reducing, and analysing 
control systems using block diagrams, signal flow graphs, root locus, and frequency response 
methods.

Stability of Linear Systems (4 Hours) 
Stability of linear systems: Definitions, characteristic polynomial and 
characteristic equation, general stability criterion with reference to s-plane; 
Absolute and relative stability; necessary conditions for stability. 
Routh’s criterion of stability.

The root-locus concept; Evan’s criteria; Construction of root locus; 
determination of roots from root locus; Effect of addition of zeros and 
poles on root locus; root contours.
