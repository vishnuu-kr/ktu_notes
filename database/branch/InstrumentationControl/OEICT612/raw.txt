Course Objectives: 
1. To develop knowledge on Micro electro mechanical systems and materials used in the 
manufacture 
2. To understand various micro fabrication procedures, techniques and packaging considerations 
 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Introduction to Micro Electronic Mechanical Systems: Materials for 
MEMS- silicon- structure and orientations, properties. Other materials- shape 
memory alloy (SMA), polymers, silicon compounds etc, material properties.  
Commercial MEMS structures and systems: 
Overview of commonly used mechanical structures in MEMS - Beams, 
Cantilevers, Plates, Diaphragms – Typical applications. Passive MEMS 
structures- fluid nozzles- ink jet nozzles. Sensors- piezoresistive pressure 
sensors- mass flow sensors- acceleration sensors, actuators- micro 
mechanical valves-micro pumps. 
 
9 
2 
Process of micromachining: Process of micromachining- pattern transfer 
using additive technique. Silicon 
growth- oxidation of silicon-molecular beam epitaxy. 
Physical vapour deposition (PVD)- sputtering- laser deposition- ion plating- 
cluster beam technology- Chemical vapour deposition (CVD)- step 
 
9 

 
 
coverage- plasma enhanced CVD, Electron cyclotron resonance CVD 
(ECRCVD)- sol-gel deposition technique. 
3 
Lithography 
Photolithography- Introduction- masks- spinning- resist and soft backing- 
exposure and post exposure treatment- development- descumming and post 
baking- resists- wafer priming, wafer cleaning and contamination- clean 
room- resist striping. Next generation lithography-extreme ultra violet 
lithography (EUVL)- x-ray lithography- LIGA- charged particle beam 
lithography- electron beam lithography- ion beam lithography. 
Etching: Introduction- dry etching- physical etching- ion etching or 
sputtering- ion beam etching, plasma etching- deep reactive ion etching 
(DRIE)- isotropic wet etching- anisotropic wet etching. 
9 
4 
MEMS packaging: Key design and packaging consideration- wafer stack 
thickness- wafer dicing concerns, thermal management- stress isolation- 
protective coating and media isolation. Wafer level packaging and die level 
packaging. Hermetic packaging- calibration and compensation- die attach 
process- solder alloy- bonding process using solder alloy- organic adhesive 
wiring and inter connections- Wire bonding- flip chip bonding- micro 
fluidics interconnections. 
Packaging material properties- mechanical- moisture penetration- interfacial 
adhesion, thermal- chemical- electrical. 
 
9

Course Type

Course Objectives: 
1. To expose the students about various instruments used in agriculture 
2. To teach the design of greenhouses using various instruments 
3. To impart ideas about modern technologies used in agriculture  
 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
  Soil Science & Sensors
Soil science :  fundamental definitions & relationships 
properties of soil :  index properties of soil,  Engineering properties 
of soil,  physical properties of soil 
permeability & seepage analysis,  Mohr’s circle of stress 
Necessity of instrumentation & control for agriculture 
Sensors and transducers: definitions,  introduction to sonic 
anemometers,  hygrometers,  thermocouples,  close path gas 
analysers,  introduction to various biosensors 
 
9 
2 
 Instrumentation in industrial Continuous & Batch processes 
Idea about continuous and batch processes 
Flow diagram of sugar plant, sensors & instrumentation set up 
Flow diagram of fermenter, sensors & instrumentation set up 
Flow diagram of dairy industry & instrumentation set up for it 
 
9 

 
 
Juice extraction control process & instrumentation set up.  
Oil extraction plant and instrumentation set up for it. 
Pesticides manufacturing process and control 
3 
  Instrumentation in Irrigation 
Necessity of irrigation in agriculture 
Evapotranspiration and its measurement methods 
irrigation methods: overhead, centre pivot, lateral move, micro 
irrigation systems & the comparison of different irrigation systems 
soil moisture measurement methods: resistance-based method, 
voltage based method, thermal based method 
Water distribution & management control, Auto drip & sprinkler 
irrigation systems,  
Irrigation canal management systems 
upstream & downstream control concept 
Introduction to SCADA , Applications of SCADA in agriculture 
11 
4 
Greenhouse Parameters & Instrumentation 
Greenhouse effect, Concept & construction of green houses, 
ventilation, cooling & heating, wind speed, temperature & 
humidity, soil moisture, rain gauge, carbon dioxide enrichment 
measurement & control 
implementation of hydraulic, pneumatic & electronics control 
circuits in harvesters cotton pickers, tractor 
Classification of pumps: pump characteristics, pump selection & 
installation. 
 
9
