Course Objectives: 
 
1. To develop a comprehensive understanding of three-phase circuit analysis 
2. To impart knowledge of S-domain analysis, transient analysis, and phasor analysis.

Three phase circuits: Phase sequence- Star and delta connection-Relation 
between line and phase voltages and currents in balanced systems Analysis of

S Domain: Transfer Function, driving point and transfer functions for one port 
and two port network. Impulse Function & Response. Concept And 
Significance Of Poles And Zeros. Time Domain Response From Pole Zero 
Plot. Concept Of Stability, Routh-Hurwitz Criterion- analysis of marginally 
stable, unstable and stable systems

Presence Of Sources. Analysis Of Source Free Networks, Transient Response

Excitations-Initial Conditions-Solution Method Using Laplace Transforms

Phasor Analysis: Application Of Phasor Analysis In RLC Networks Filters:
Low Pass, High Pass, Band Pass, Band Elimination, Filter Design and

Course Outcomes (COs) 
 
At the end of the course students should be able to: 
 
 
Course Outcome 
Bloom’s 
Knowledge 
Level (KL) 
 
CO1 
Analyze and solve problems related to three-phase circuits, including the 
relationships between line and phase voltages and currents in balanced 
systems, and measure active and reactive power . 
 
K3 
 
CO2 
Apply S-domain analysis techniques to determine transfer functions, 
understand the significance of poles and zeros, and derive time-domain 
responses from pole-zero plots to assess system behavior. 
 
K3 
 
CO3 
Evaluate the stability of electrical systems using the Routh-Hurwitz 
criterion and categorize systems as stable, marginally stable, or unstable
based on this analysis. 
 
K3 
 
CO4 
Perform AC transient analysis of R-L, R-C, and R-L-C circuits under 
sinusoidal excitations using Laplace transforms, and apply phasor 
analysis for the effective study of RLC networks, including the design 
and implementation of various types of filters.
