Course Objectives: 
1. Introduce fundamental concepts of digital control systems, including discrete-time system 
representation, sampling processes, and the effects of sampling periods on system 
behavior. 
2.  Provide methods for modeling discrete-time systems, focusing on Z-transforms, s-plane 
to z-plane mapping, and stability analysis techniques such as Jury stability test and bi-
linear transformation. 
3. Develop skills for designing digital control systems using frequency response plots, root 
locus plots, and z-plane synthesis, addressing both transient and steady-state responses. 
4. Explore discrete state-space models, including state variable representations, stability 
analysis via Lyapunov's theorem, and advanced design techniques such as pole 
placement, observer design, and state feedback design.
