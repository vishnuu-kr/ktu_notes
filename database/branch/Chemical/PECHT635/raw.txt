Course Objectives: 
1. Impart a solid understanding of the fundamental principles of fluid dynamics through 
governing equations, turbulence models and boundary conditions. 
2. Develop the ability to analyze and solve fluid flow problems using numerical techniques. 
3. Enable proficient use of computational tools to set up, run, and interpret simulations for a 
variety of fluid flow problems, ensuring application to real-world engineering scenarios and 
research projects.

Suggestions on Project Topics 
Assignment/Project: 20 Marks 
Students should evaluate and analyze a real-world problem, assess the proposed solutions, 
provide a conclusion on which solution is most appropriate for the problem, and implement 
the chosen solution using any computational tools like ANSYS, Open Foam, Matlab etc. 
Level: Analyze 
1. Problem Definition and Decomposition: 
 Clarity and Completeness (2 points): Evaluate the clarity and completeness of the 
problem definition. Are the objectives well-defined? Is the problem broken down into 
manageable sub-problems? 
 
Identification of Key Parameters (2 points): Assess the identification and justification of 
key parameters influencing the fluid dynamics problem. Are all relevant variables and 
boundary conditions considered? 
2. Analytical Approach: 
 
Methodological Soundness (1 points): Evaluate the soundness of the chosen analytical 
methods. Are the equations and principles correctly applied to the problem? 
 
Critical Analysis (1 points): Assess the depth of critical analysis in the approach. Are 
assumptions and limitations clearly stated and justified? 
 

 
3. Data Interpretation: 
 
Accuracy and Consistency (2 points): Evaluate the accuracy and consistency of the data 
interpretation. Are the results logically derived from the data? 
 
Comparison with Theoretical Predictions (2 points): Assess how well the results are 
compared with theoretical predictions or existing literature. Are discrepancies analyzed 
and explained? 
Level: Evaluate 
1. Simulation Setup and Execution: 
 
Appropriateness of CFD Tool (1 point): Evaluate the appropriateness of the chosen CFD 
tool for the problem at hand. Is the tool well-suited to model the specific fluid dynamics 
scenario? 
 
Simulation Accuracy and Robustness (2 points): Assess the accuracy and robustness of 
the simulation setup. Are the mesh, time steps, and convergence criteria appropriately 
chosen? 
2. Results Validation: 
 
Validation against Experimental Data (1 point): Evaluate the extent and rigor of 
validation against experimental or benchmark data. Are the validation methods 
appropriate and convincingly presented? 
 
Error Analysis (1 point): Assess the thoroughness of error analysis. Are sources of error 
identified and quantified? Are their impacts on the results discussed? 
3. Conclusions and Recommendations: 
 
Logical Consistency (1 point): Evaluate the logical consistency of the conclusions drawn 
from the analysis and simulation results. Are the conclusions supported by the data? 
 
Practical Implications and Recommendations (1 point): Assess the practical 
implications and recommendations based on the findings. Are the recommendations 
feasible and well-grounded in the analysis? 
4. Presentation and Communication: 
 
Clarity and Organization (1 point): Evaluate the clarity and organization of the project 
report or presentation. Is the information logically structured and easy to follow? 
 
Technical Writing and Visual Aids (2 points): Assess the quality of technical writing 
and the effectiveness of visual aids (graphs, charts, simulations). Are complex concepts 
clearly explained and visually supported?
