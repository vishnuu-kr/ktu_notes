Hours 
1 
Artificial Intelligence: Types of problems AI addresses like Computer 
Vision, Natural Language Processing, Robotics, Expert Systems, Object 
detection and Image segmentation.  
Artificial Intelligence in Chemical Engineering Background: Early Attempts, 
Expert System Era, Neural Network Era, Effects and Lacunae of Expert 
System Era and Neural Network Era on Chemical Engineering , Deep 
Learning and Data Science Era. 
Applications of AI in chemical engineering: areas like fault diagnosis, 
Process control, Process design, development and selection like Separation 
Design, Heat-Exchanger Network Synthesis, Thermodynamic Model 
Selection and Physical Property Estimation, Oil reservoir image 
segmentation. 
Early applications of AI techniques in chemical engineering. The FALCON, 
CATDEX, BIOEXPERT and the ExSep algorithms. 
9 
2 
Introduction to Machine Learning: Exploring sub-discipline of AI: 
Machine 
Learning, 
Supervised 
learning, 
Unsupervised 
learning, 
Reinforcement learning, Classification problems, Regression problems, 
Clustering problems, Introduction to neural networks and deep learning. 
Deep Artificial neural networks (DNN): The working of the Rosenblatt 
perceptron, multilayer perceptrons. Activation functions and their 
9 

 
importance in incorporating nonlinearities into the predictive models. The 
feedforward process in ANN layers with dense fully connected layers. The 
error (loss) functions as a measure of the ANN performance. 
Backpropagation algorithm for neuron learning.  
3 
Convolution neural networks (CNN): The drawbacks of Deep ANN. How 
CNN take into account the spatial patterns. The working of the CNN in 
pattern recognition. The role of kernels, pooling, padding and stride in CNN 
learning. How the kernels help in reducing the learning parameters (weight 
sharing). Some outstanding deep networks proposed like AlexNet, VGGNet, 
Inception, Google Net and ResNets. The problems they faced and how they 
resolved the problems. 
9 
4 
Case Studies: Oxidative coupling of methane (OCM) over the 
Mn/Na2WO4/SiO2 catalyst in a fluidized bed reactor, Control of Reactive 
Distillation Column, Use of Qualitative Models in Discrete Event Simulation 
to Analyze Malfunctions in Processing Systems, Fault Detection and 
Diagnosis Using Artificial Neural Networks, A Modular Approach to 
Multiple Faults Diagnosis, AI in Chemical Engineering Recent Trends and 
Future Outlook: Emergence in large scale systems of self-organizing 
intelligent agents.
