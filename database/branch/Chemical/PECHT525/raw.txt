Course Objectives: 
1. Enable the students to model chemical engineering problems. 
2. Equip the students with knowledge on modelling and simulation of 
processes and use various concepts such as linear algebra, differential 
calculus etc. to solve chemical engineering problems.

Criteria for Evaluation (Evaluate and Analyse): 20 marks 
 
1. Problem Definition (4 points) 
1. Clearly defines the real-world problem in process engineering. 
2. Examine and identifies relevant contextual factors. 
2. Problem Analysis (4 points) 
1. Break-down and presents a well-reasoned solution approach. 
2. Compare and justify the proposed solutions with evidence and logical reasoning. 
3. Evaluate (4 points) 
1. Thoroughly evaluate the proposed solutions. 
2. Considers feasibility and practical implications. 
4. Implementation of software (4 points) 
1. Select the most feasible solution by implementing the proposed solutions. 
2. Demonstrates proficiency in relevant software. 
5. Conclusion (K4- 2 points, K5 – 2 points) 
1. Summarizes findings and insights. (K4) 
2. Reflects critical thinking and informed decision-making. (K5)
