Course Objectives: 
1. To impart a comprehensive study of polymers and their classification, Molecular Weight 
and Properties, synthesis and characterization, processing methods and engineering 
application of polymers

Introduction to polymers-Definition and classification of polymers, 
classification of polymer based on source, structure, application, thermal 
behaviour, mode of polymerization - natural and synthetic polymers, 
thermoplastics, thermosets, elastomers, basic concepts of polymerization 
(addition, condensation), Copolymerisation-Different types of copolymers-
Characteristics.

2 
Molecular weight of polymers – weight average and number average 
molecular weight – sedimentation and viscosity average molecular weights. 
Experimental methods for molecular weight determination – end group 
analysis, light scattering method – viscometry (Ostwald viscometer) intrinsic 
viscosity. Molecular weight distribution curve.  
Polymer properties - Polymer architecture (linear, branched, crosslinked, 
etc.), Crystallinity and glass transition temperature, Mechanical properties, 
(elasticity, viscoelasticity, yield), Thermal properties and degradation, 
Electrical and optical properties

Polymer processing and engineering applications-  
Plastic processing technology-injection moulding-compression moulding - 
calendaring – blow moulding – extrusion – thermoforming – wet, dry and 
melt spinning methods for fibres –vulcanization of rubber –– general study 
of elastomer processing - brief description of compounding methods. 
Applications in drug delivery, tissue engineering, and medical devices, 
conductive polymers and their applications, polymers in microelectronics 
and photonics, dielectric and insulating materials,  polymers in automotive 
and aerospace applications, polymer coatings and adhesives
