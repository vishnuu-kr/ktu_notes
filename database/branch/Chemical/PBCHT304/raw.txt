Course Objectives: 
1. Introduce the concepts of fluid and particle mechanics and its relevance in Chemical 
Engineering.  
2. Impart the fundamental principles of momentum transport for systems with and without 
particles.  
3. Develop the ability to apply principles of fluid and particle mechanics for the design of 
process flow systems

Suggestion on Project Topics 
 
Project based learning (8hrs) 
 
 The students shall be directed to design a part of flow network consisting of 
combinations pipe sections, fittings, flow measuring devices from an appropriate 
process flow diagram. 
 The flow system selected may fall into either of the two category such as internal 
flow and external flow systems. 
 The design developed should include the following  concepts: 
1. The relevance of transport properties in designing the system as studied in  
module 1 
2. The principles of pressure measurement using the concepts of hydrostatic 
equilibrium learned in module 1. 
3. The type of flow such as laminar or turbulent. 
4. How to verify the continuity, momentum, and energy balance equations with 
reference to the selected flow system as learned in the module 2. 
5. The use of pumping devices in flow systems. 
6. How to calculate the pressure drop in the selected flow system. 
7. The use of friction factor in calculating the pressure drop induced by various 
pipe fitting and auxiliaries as learned in the module 3. 
8. The working of various flow measuring devices used in flow system and the at 
least one flow measuring device should be there in the selected system. 
9. How to calculate the pressure drop in flow system falls into the category of 
external flow as learned in the module 4.  
10. The pressure drop estimation in packed and fluidized beds. 
How to do the modelling and simulation of such simple systems selected using any appropriate 
software/ Experimental validation of such system.
