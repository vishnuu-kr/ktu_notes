Course Objectives: 
 Sound knowledge in process control is very important for Chemical Engineers. Process control has 
become increasingly important in the process industries as a consequence of global competition, 
rapidly changing economic conditions, and more stringent environmental and safety regulations. 
Proper application of process control improves the safety and profitability of a process, while 
maintaining consistently the desired product quality. 
1. Model chemical engineering processes and analyze/predict their dynamics both for open-
(without control) and closed-loop (with control) cases. 
2. Develop control strategies and select the most appropriate input to manipulate, and totune 
controllers to meet/achieve process objectives.

Suggestion on Project Topics 
The students shall be directed to carry out any one of the projects listed below. 
1. Design and simulation of suitable temperature control system for stirred tank heater. 
2. Design and simulation of suitable level or flow control system for a single tank system. 
3. Design and simulationof suitable level or flow control system for a multi-tank system. 
4. Design and simulation of suitable control system to maintain product concentration in 
continuous stirred tank reactor. 
5. Design and simulation of a temperature control system for a continuous stirred tank reactor 
used in chemical process. 
6. Any other relevant project related to process dynamics and control. 
 

 
 
 
The completed project work should consist of following key components 
 
1. Fundamental Concept:  The dynamic behaviour of the process (Module 1) 
2. System Modelling: Develop a model for the selected system from fundamental 
conservation principles  (Module 1 and 2) 
3. Block Diagram development: Develop a feed back control system using the concept of 
block diagram development and reduction. (Module 3) 
4. Controller selection: Select a suitable controller for the process (Module 2 and 3) 
5. Simulation: Implement the system model and controller in MATLAB or Simulink and 
simulate the response. (Self -study) 
6. Stability analysis: Analyse the stability of the system using methods like Routh-Hurwitz 
Criterion, Root Locus method, Bode stability criterion etc. (Module 4) 
7. Controller tuning: Optimize the control parameter for desired performance using 
Ziegler- Nichols tuning. (Module 4) 
8. Validation by experimental data: Conduct experiment in the laboratory and evaluate the 
system performance with the experimental data (Conduct experiment in lab and obtain 
data for validation) 
9. Report preparation and oral presentation: Prepare a detailed report and presentation 
before the evaluation committee.
