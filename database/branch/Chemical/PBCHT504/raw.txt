Course Objectives: 
1. To give undergraduates a foundation in designing equipment used in process industries 
for heat transfer and evaporation operations. 
2. To familiarise using codes and standards for designing process equipment.

1 
Thermal design of double pipe heat exchanger: Constructional features - 
Calculation of heat transfer area – estimation of heat transfer coefficient – 
calculation of the size of double pipe – pressure drop estimation in double 
pipe flow. 
Thermal design of shell and tube heat exchanger: Constructional features 
– baffles and pass partition – TEMA nomenclature – routing of fluids - 
Calculation of heat transfer area – estimation of tube bundle size - estimation 
of overall heat transfer coefficient using Bell’s method – pressure drop in 
shell side and tube side. 
Thermal design of shell and tube condensers for single component 
isothermal condensation of saturated vapours in the shell. Estimation of 
heat transfer area – Estimation of bundle size and shell diameter - estimation 
of in-shell condensation coefficients using Dukler charts/Nusselt equation – 
estimation of overall heat transfer coefficient.

2 
Thermal design of evaporators: 
Estimation of heat transfer area in single effect and multiple 
effect(double/triple effect forward feed only) evaporators with negligible 
boiling point rise   
Effect of boiling point elevation in evaporator calculation.  Constructional 
features and design of short tube calandria evaporator, long tube evaporator, 
and forced circulation evaporator. 
Mechanical design of process equipment: Design of thin-walled pressure 
vessels as per IS 2825 –Shells subject to internal and external pressure- flat 
ends, ends concave to pressure, and convex to pressure: domed ends and 
conical ends.

Suggestions on Project Topics 
 Identify a tubular exchanger in a plant and design the exchanger. The design shall include the 
following: 
a. Selection of exchanger type with justification 
b. Selection of material of construction for various parts, its specification with 
approximate cost data. 
c. Obtain/Prepare physical properties for the fluids in the operating range required for 
design. 
d. Manual sizing- First Design. 
e. Use computational tools to optimize the first design. 
f. Prepare design data sheet as per standards. 
 
 Identify a process that uses different types of evaporators. Choose a suitable evaporator and 
design it. The design shall include the following: 
g. Selection of evaporator type with justification 
h. Selection of material of construction for various parts, its specification with 
approximate cost data. 
i. 
Obtain/Prepare physical properties for the fluids in the operating range required for 
design. 
j. 
Manual sizing- First Design. 
k. Use computational tools to optimize the first design. 
l. 
Prepare design data sheet as per standards. 
 

 
 
 Conduct the mechanical design of tubular exchangers and evaporators as per Indian Standards/ 
ASME and prepare drawings.

any, operating conditions, and materials handled. Data required for design such as 
equilibrium data and physical properties in case it cannot be obtained from
