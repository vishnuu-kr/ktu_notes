Course Objectives: 
1. To explain basic principles and theories of heterogeneous catalysis, including adsorption, 
desorption, surface reactions, and diffusion processes. 
2. To develop an understanding of reaction kinetics in heterogeneous 
catalysis. 
3. To acquire skills in various techniques for catalyst preparation and characterisation.

Hours 
1 
Catalyst and catalytic reactors: Catalysts, types of catalysts, catalytic 
properties, steps in a catalytic reaction, adsorption equilibrium constant, 
desorption, surface reaction, synthesizing rate law, rate limiting step, 
Langmuir-Hinshelwood approach. Development of design equations for 
ideal mixed batch reactor, plug flow tubular reactor and perfectly mixed 
continuous stirred tank reactor for heterogeneous systems. Heterogeneous 
data analysis for reactor design 
9 
2 
Diffusion and reaction in porous catalysts: effective diffusivity, 
tortuosity-modelling of diffusion with reaction on a spherical catalysts. 
Thiele Modulus, internal effectiveness factor, Overall effectiveness factor. 
Estimation of diffusion and reaction limited regimes - Weisz - Prater 
criterion for internal diffusion, Mears criterion for external diffusion. 
9 

 
3 
Fluid Particle Reactions (Non catalytic):  Selection of a model: 
Unreacted core model for spherical particles of unchanging size, model 
development for diffusion through gas film, ash layer, and chemical 
reaction controls. Rate of reaction for shrinking spherical particles - 
chemical reaction controls, diffusion controls, application to design., 
9 
4 
Selection, design, and Preparation of catalysts:  Deposition, impregnation, 
precipitation, co-precipitation, and sol-gel formation. Characterization of 
catalysts- pore volume, pore size, and pore size distribution- Mercury 
porosimetry- Nitrogen desorption method- BET method. Thermogravimetric 
analysis- Differential thermal analysis- X-ray diffraction method- Electron
