Course Objectives: 
1. Impart the basic concepts of safety in Process Industries. 
2. Equip students with the skills of  risk assessment techniques and hazard identification 
3. Develop an understanding about Consequence analysis and Quantitative risk assessment 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Introduction to process safety, Process safety strategies- Inherent, Passive, 
Active and Procedural, Layer of protection, Safety integrity level. Hazard, 
Risk, Chemical hazards-Fire, Explosion, Toxic gas release.  
Dow fire & Explosion index, Toxicity index, Chemical explosion index, 
Inventory analysis.  Major industrial hazards, Reasons. 
9 
2 
Techniques for hazard Identification;- safety audit, Safety inspection, 
Preliminary hazard analysis, HAZOP, What if analysis, Fault tree analysis, 
Event tree analysis, Failure mode and effect analysis, Layer of protection 
analysis, Bow-tie analysis (methodology and examples). 
9 
3 
Consequence analysis- Steps involved in the consequence analysis, 
Consequence of fire, explosion and toxic gas release.  Source modelling, 
Models for fire- pool fire, Models for explosion- TNT equivalent, Models for 
dispersion- Gaussian gas dispersion model. Probit equation for fire, explosion 
and toxic gas release. 
9 
4 
Quantitative risk assessment- Procedure for QRA, Risk, type of risk, 
acceptable risk-ALARP, representation of risk. Calculation of Individual risk, 
Societal risk, FN Curve. Risk reduction strategies.   
Emergency planning- Onsite and offsite emergency plan, Reliability-way to 
improve reliability. Tools for inherent safety, Inherent safety indices.
