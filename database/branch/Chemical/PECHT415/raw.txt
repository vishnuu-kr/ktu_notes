Course Objectives: 
 
1. Impart the basic concepts of fluidization phenomenon 
2. Familiarize the fundamental and practical aspects of fluidization operations for 
industrial application.

Hours 
 
 
 
 
1 
The phenomenon of fluidization – types of fluidization, liquid like behaviour 
of a f1uidized bed, advantages and disadvantages of fluidized bed for 
industrial operations, fluidization quality, selection of contacting mode for 
given application. 
Minimum fluidization velocity, pressure drop versus velocity diagram – 
uniformly sized particles, concept of minimum bubbling velocity, effect of 
temperature and pressure on fluidized bed behaviour, sintering and 
agglomeration of particles at high temperature. 
Geldart classification of particles, mapping of fluidization regimes. 
 
Microproject/assignment – calculation of particle size distribution, minimum 
fluidization velocity and pressure drop 
 
 
 
 
 
11 
 
 
2 
Distributors: types of distributors – ideal distributors, perforated plates, 
Tuyeres and caps, pipe grids and spargers, gas entry region of a bed, gas jets 
in fluidized beds, pressure drop requirements across distributors. Power 
consumption. Entrainment and elutriation – concept of FBH, TDH. 
Solid movement – vertical and horizontal movement, segregation of solid 
particles, large solids in beds of smaller particles, staging of fluidized beds. 
Microproject/assignment/self-study- Design of distributor 
 
 
11 

 
 
 
 
 
 
3 
Bubbles in dense beds: single rising bubbles, rise rate of bubbles, Davidson 
model for gas flow at bubbles, wake region, solids within bubbles (overview 
only), coalescence and splitting of bubbles, bubble formation above 
distributor. Slug flow 
Bubbling fluidized bed: estimation of bed properties – gas flow in the 
emulsion phase, bubble gas flow, bubble size and bubble growth, bubble rise 
velocity. K-L flow model for bubbling fluidized bed. Gas dispersion and gas 
interchange in bubbling beds (concept only) 
Microproject/ assignment/self-study - Simulation/modeling of bubbling 
fluidized bed. 
 
 
 
 
11 
 
 
 
 
 
4 
Particle- to- gas mass and heat transfer: Mass transfer – single spheres 
and fixed bed and gas fluidized bed. Interpretation of mass transfer 
coefficients. Heat transfer – single spheres and fixed bed and gas fluidized 
bed. Interpretation of heat transfer coefficients. 
Industrial application of fluidized beds: 
Physical operations: Heat exchange, coating of metal objects with plastic, 
drying of solids, adsorption. 
Synthesis reaction: Fischer–Tropsch synthesis, polymerization of olefins. 
Cracking: Fluid catalytic cracking (FCC), Fluid coking and Flexi coking, 
Thermal cracking 
Fluidized combustion of coal, gasification of coal and coke, calcination. 
 
Microproject/assignment/self-study – fitting reported mass transfer and heat 
transfer data with the bubbling bed model

Criteria for Evaluation (Evaluate and Analyse): 20 marks 
 
1. Problem Definition (4 points) 
a. Clearly defines the real-world problem involving application of fluidization. 
b. Examine and identifies relevant factors influencing the process. 
2. Problem Analysis (5 points) 
a. Present a well-reasoned solution approach. 
b. Compare and justify the proposed solutions with evidence and logical reasoning. 
3. Evaluate (5 points) 
a. Thoroughly evaluate the proposed solutions, considering feasibility and practical 
implications. 
4. Implementation of software (2 points)

5. Conclusion (K4- 2 points, K5 – 2 points) 
a. Summarizes findings and insights. (K4)
