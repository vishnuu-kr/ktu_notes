Course Objectives: 
1. To make the students aware of different forms of energy and methods for harnessing 
different energy resources. 
2. To understand the importance of energy conservation and the different methods employed 
for energy conservation in industry

Energy:- general classification of energy, world energy resources, 
energy consumption and future perspectives, Indian energy resources 
energy alternatives, Need of clean and renewable energy. 
Conventional energy plants:- Thermal, hydel and nuclear power 
plants, efficiency, merits and demerits of the above power plants, 
combined cycle power plants

Solar energy:- solar insolation and properties, solar thermal systems, 
flat plate collectors, focusing collectors, solar water 
heating, solar cooling, solar distillation, solar refrigeration, solar 
dryers, solar pond, solar thermal power generation, photo voltaic 
systems , energy plantations, solar energy application in India 
Ocean energy:- Ocean wave energy conversion, ocean thermal energy 
conversion, tidal energy conversion 
Geothermal energy:- Applications and mechanism for electricity 
production

3 
Wind energy:-Types of windmills, types of wind rotors, wind electric 
power generation, wind power in India, nature of wind and site 
selection, economics of wind farm 
Biomass energy:- Resources, thermo-chemical and biochemical 
methods of biomass conversion:- combustion, gasification, pyrolysis, 
fermentation, anaerobic digestion 
Magneto hydro dynamics: open cycle and closed cycle systems.

Fuel cells:- alkaline fuel cell, phosphoric acid fuel cell, molten 
carbonate fuel cell, solid oxide fuel cell, solid polymer electrolyte fuel 
cell, Basic concepts of Microbial Fuel Cell 
Energy from Hydrogen :- Hydrogen as a source of energy, Sources, 
hydrogen energy storage, Applications, Future perspectives 
Energy Conservation: - Energy audit and Energy conservation in 
chemical process plants, Energy conservation in petroleum, fertilizer 
and steel industries. 
Modern Technologies in Energy Systems: - Cogeneration (CHP),
