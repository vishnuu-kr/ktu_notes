Course Objectives: 
1. Impart the fundamental principles and concepts of process integration and its importance in 
enhancing efficiency and sustainability in industrial processes.  
2. Apply energy targeting methods to determine the minimum energy requirements and design 
heat exchanger networks by applying pinch analysis.  
3. Develop skills in design of mass exchanger networks to achieve efficient mass transfer and 
resource utilization within industrial systems. 
4. Apply comprehensive process integration techniques to Integrate reactor systems and 
distillation columns.

Criteria for evaluation: 
Level: Analyze 
 
1. Problem Definition (2 marks) 
Choose an industrial plant or a section of a plant. Gather detailed information about 
the chosen plant or unit, including process flow diagrams, operating conditions, and 
energy/mass balances. 
2. Problem Analysis (5 marks) 
Define the goal of the project. Collect necessary data such as temperatures, flow rates, 
specific heats etc. 
3. Identifying Software (3 marks) 

 
Identify the different software tools required to implement the work and learn its 
usage.
