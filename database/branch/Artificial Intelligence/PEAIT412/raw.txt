Course Objectives:
1. To provide a comprehensive understanding of data warehousing concepts, architecture,
and the role of dimensional modeling in designing data warehouses.
2. To equip students with practical skills in ETL processes, OLAP operations, and
optimizing data warehouses for real-world applications.

Overview of Data Warehousing - Need, Definition, Characteristics,
Benefits, Operational vs. Analytical Systems, Applications in Industry.

Dimensional Modeling & Design :-
Dimensional Modeling Basics - Facts, Dimensions, Star Schema,
Snowflake Schema; Steps in Dimensional Modeling; Fact Tables,
Dimension Tables; Data Warehouse Design-Top-Down & Bottom-Up,
Fact Table Granularity; Slowly Changing Dimensions; Advances in
Dimensional Modeling - Aggregates & Roll-Up, Time Dimension, Junk
Dimensions, Degenerate Dimensions.

ETL Processes & Tools :-
ETL Concepts and Process - Extracting Data from Multiple Sources,
Data Transformation and Cleansing, Loading Data; ETL Tools and
Platforms - Common ETL Tools (Informatica, Talend, Microsoft SSIS),
Scheduling and Automation of ETL Processes; Data Quality and Data
Governance in ETL - Handling Incomplete and Inconsistent Data,
Ensuring Data Accuracy, Consistency, and Integrity.
[Text 1 : Ch 4-7]

Partitioning, Performance Tuning; OLAP - Architectures: MOLAP,
ROLAP, HOLAP;

Maintenance - Data Loading Automation, Backup, and Recovery, Data
Warehouse Evolution and Scalability.
