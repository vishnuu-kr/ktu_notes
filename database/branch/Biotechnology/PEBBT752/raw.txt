Course Objectives:  
At the end of the course, students should be able to 
1. Define the molecular, cellular, and tissue-level organization of the central and peripheral  
2. nervous system. Relate the properties of individual cells to their function in organized neural 
circuits and systems  
3. Outline the properties of cells that make up the nervous system including the propagation of  
4. electrical signals used for cellular communication  
5. Restate the basics of neural pathology and pathogenesis.  
6. Identify the basic concepts of the mind and brain that define the discipline of cognitive 
science.  
7. Formulate a research question and design an original research plan to address an original 
research question
