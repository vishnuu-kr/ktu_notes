Course Objectives: 
1. To introduce the principles and basic concepts of Remote Sensing and GIS   
2. To introduce the remote sensing systems, data products and analysis 
3. To introduce concepts of GIS  and map projections 
4. To study the applications of Remote Sensing and GIS in agriculture, soil and water resources

CONCEPTS OF REMOTE SENSING AND SATELLITES 
Definition- Historical background - Components of remote sensing – Energy 
source, electromagnetic spectrum, radiation laws, platforms and sensors - 
Active and passive remote sensing satellites - Types - Sun synchronous - Geo 
synchronous remote sensing satellites - Resolutions - Spectral, spatial, 
radiometric and Temporal resolution- LANDSAT, Sentinel  and  IRS – Data 
acquisition -  Concept of spectral radiance, Digital Number and Reflectance- 
Energy interaction with earth surface feature-Atmospheric effects on remote

DATA PRODUCTS AND IMAGE ANALYSIS 
Data products –based on level of processing- o/p – scale – area/coverage – 
data availability - Image interpretation – Visual interpretation elements – 
interpretation key. Digital image processing – Image enhancement – image 
classification – Supervised and unsupervised.

CONCEPTS OF GIS 
History and development of GIS – Definition – Components of GIS –  
Characteristics of Maps – Elements – Map scale, Vector and Raster data 
models- Attribute data -Map Projections, Coordinate systems – Sources of 
spatial Data-Spatial analysis in GIS-Spatial interpolation using GIS.

APPLICATION OF RS AND GIS 
Crop Acreage estimation - Estimation of Crop Water Requirement using 
thermal remote sensing – Crop condition - Soil mapping – classification of 
soil with digital numbers – soil erosion mapping- reservoir sedimentation 
using image processing - Inventory of water resources – water quality 
assessment - Application of Remote Sensing and GIS in Precision 
Agriculture - Monitor Crop Health - Management Decision Support Systems
