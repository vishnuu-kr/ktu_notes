Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the basic concept of machine design. 
2. To understand the strength and stress failures of different joints.  
3. To learn the design of shaft, keys, belts and gears. 
4. To understand the design considerations of tillage implements. 
5. To understand the design considerations of sowing, harvesting and threshing equipment / machines

Syllabus Description

Hours 
1 
Understanding of design concepts, Phases of design, design considerations. 
Common engineering materials and their mechanical properties. Types of 
loads and stresses, Failure theories, factor of safety, selection of allowable 
stress. Stress concentration. Elementary fatigue and creep aspects. Cotter 
joints, knuckle joint and pinned joints, turnbuckle. Design of welded joints 
subjected to static loads. 
 
9  
2 
Design of threaded fasteners subjected to direct static loads, bolted joints 
loaded in shear and bolted joints subjected to eccentric loading. Design of 
shafts under torsion and combined bending and torsion. Design of keys. 
Design of lever, Design of Jaw coupling, sleeve, and rigid flange couplings. 
Design of helical and leaf springs. Design of flat belt and V-belt drives and 
pulleys. 
 
9  

 
 
3 
Design of gears- Spur gear, Design of screw motion mechanisms like screw 
jack, lead screw, etc. Selection of anti-friction bearings -Ball bearings  
Design considerations for tillage implements such as ploughs, harrows and 
cultivators. Design of mould board plough, analysis of forces acting on 
plough bottom by the cut soil slices,  
Disc implement types and design of disc for different disc tools, spacing of 
disc in multi-disc tools. Force analysis of vertical and inclined disc tools. 
Specific work of rotary tools and machine and dynamic forces. Reliability 
criteria in design and its application. 
9 
4 
Design consideration of sowing and planting equipment, design of seed 
metering mechanism, design parameters of seed and fertilizer box and frame, 
design of seed metering mechanism of planters. Fertilizers handling 
equipment and their design parameters and requirements. 
Design consideration of threshing and harvesting equipment with reference to 
types of cylinder and safety measures. 
Design and selection of matching power unit. Safety devices and ergonomical 
considerations for the design and development of tractors & farm implements.
