Course Objectives: 
1. To equip students with the necessary knowledge and skills essential for addressing the dynamic 
challenges in the agricultural sector.  
2. To embark the students on a journey that spans from foundational principles to advanced 
concepts, preparing them for a future where sustainable and efficient water management in 
agriculture is paramount.

Basic Principles of Fluid Mechanics-Introduction to Fluid Mechanics- 
Fundamentals of fluid properties. Types of flow: laminar, turbulent, and 
transitional- Bernoulli's equation and its relevance to pump systems. Flow in 
Pipes- Pipe friction losses and head calculations - Reynolds number and its 
significance - Hydraulic gradients in pump systems. Cavitation: causes, 
effects, and prevention- Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH) and its 
importance. Introduction to Pump Systems- Importance of pump systems in 
agriculture-Types of pumping systems in agriculture- Types of Pumps and 
Their Applications- Centrifugal pumps: working principles and applications 
- Positive displacement pumps: reciprocating and rotary pumps- Specialized

hydraulic control in agricultural machinery- Overview of hydraulic

and Schematics- Control Valves and Their Types-Classification of Hydraulic 
Control Valves- Flow Control in Hydraulic Systems-Pressure Regulation in 
Hydraulic Systems.

Automation in Pump Control Systems- Programmable Logic Controllers 
(PLCs) in pump control- Remote sensing and monitoring of pump 
parameters- Sustainable pump technologies and eco-friendly materials- 
Anticipating and adapting to future developments.

Course Objectives: 
1. This course is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of organic 
semiconductors and action of various layers used in organic devices. 
2. The students will gain the skills necessary to design and develop organic devices like 
OLEDs, OFETs and OPVs after the completion of the course. 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Basics of Organic semiconductor, conduction mechanism in organic 
semiconductors, Pi and Sigma electron band theory. Fundamentals of 
conducting polymers. Charge transport in conjugated polymers. Effect of 
solitons. Conduction mechanism in doped polymeric semiconductors. 
Luminescence, injection and transports properties in organic semiconductors. 
9 
2 
Organic light emitting diodes(OLED) and Polymer light emitting diodes 
(PLED) – Basic device architecture, Single layer architecture and multilayer 
architecture, electrical properties and operating characteristics, Flexible 
display device architecture, Fabrication and characterization, Principle and 
architecture of organic transistors. 
9 
3 
Basic principle and architecture of Plastic Solar cell. Charge transport, effects 
of excitation diffusion, dissociation and luminescence, Photo-generation 
9 

 
 
process in organic heterojunction, photovoltaic cells. Processing of organic 
solar cells. Dye sensitization – dye sensitized solar cell. 
4 
Essential characteristics and conductivity of electrode materials in organo 
electronic devices, Effect of Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) oxidative properties on 
efficiency and shelf life of organic electronic devices, Buffer organic layer 
protection to the active layer. Doping and for increased efficiency and shelf 
life– architecture. 
Devices fabrication- Thin film coating technique. Spin coating, dip coating, 
doctor blading screen printing, inkjet printing, vapor deposition. R.F and 
microwave plasma assisted film coating.
