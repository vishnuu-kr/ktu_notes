Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the basic principles and fundamental concepts of fluid flow 
2. To impart knowledge on static, kinematics and dynamic aspects of Fluid Mechanics 
3. To understand hydraulics of flow through pipes and open channels 
4. To acquire knowledge on hydraulic machines such as pumps and turbines

Introduction and basic concepts; Properties of fluids- density, specific 
weight, vapour pressure, viscosity, Newton’s law of viscosity, surface 
tension and capillarity, ideal and real fluid; Pressure and its measurement- 
fluid pressure at a point, Pascal’s law, pressure variation in a fluid at a point, 
gauge pressure, absolute pressure, vacuum pressure; Measurement of 
pressure-piezometer, manometers -simple U tube, differential and single 
column manometers and mechanical pressure gauges.; Fluid statics- total 
pressure and centre of pressure, pressure forces on plane and curved 
surfaces,  buoyancy and floatation- expression for buoyant force and centre 
of buoyancy, metacentre and meta centric height- determination of 
metacentric height-experimental and analytical method; Principle of flotation 
and stability of floating and submerged bodies.

2 
Kinematics of fluid flow: Eulerian and Lagrangian approaches,  types of 
fluid flow- 1- D, 2-D and 3-D flow, steady, unsteady, uniform, non-
uniform, laminar, turbulent, rotational, irrotational flows, translation, 
rotation, circulation and vorticity, vortex motion-free vortex and forced 
vortex; continuity equation-continuity equation in three dimensions, 
velocity and acceleration; streamlines, path lines, streak lines, stream 
function, velocity potential and flow net; Dynamics of fluid flow – Eulers 
and Bernoulli’s equation of motion, application of Bernoulli’s equation-
venturi meter, Orifice meter, nozzle meter and pitot tube; Other flow 
measuring devices-orifices and mouthpieces, notches and weirs- 
classification, flow through them and hydraulic coefficients; Energies in 
flowing fluid- pressure head, dynamic, static and total head. 
11 
3 
Flow through pipes – laminar and turbulent flow, head loss due to friction- 
major energy losses- Darcy Weisbach equation, Chezy’s equation, 
Moodys chart, Minor losses- Head loss due to pipe fittings (only 
description); Hydraulic gradient and total energy line; flow through 
network of pipes- pipes in series, pipes in parallel, equivalent pipe; Power 
transmission through pipes; siphon, Water hammer and Cavitation 
(Description only). Open channel flow-types of flow in channels, velocity 
distribution, Measurement of velocity in channels- current meter, parshall 
flumes, cut-throat flumes and venturi flumes, Chezy’s and Manning’s 
equation; Most economical section of channels-rectangular, trapezoidal, 
triangular and circular channel; Specific energy, critical depth and 
hydraulic jump.
