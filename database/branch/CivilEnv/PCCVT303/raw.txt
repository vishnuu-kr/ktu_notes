Course Objectives: 
1. To analyse both statically determinate and indeterminate structures using elastic and plastic theory 
2. To analyse SDOF systems subjected to harmonic loading.

Analysis of Statically Indeterminate Structures: 
Degree of static and kinematic indeterminacies; Introduction to force 
and displacement methods. Method of consistent deformations: 
Analysis of beams (simple problems with one redundant, illustration

3 
Cables: Analysis of forces in cables under concentrated and 
uniformly distributed loads; Arches: Theory of arches – Eddy’s 
theorem; Analysis of three-hinged arches; Normal thrust and radial 
shear due to simple cases of loading 
Introduction to Plastic Theory. Approximate methods of analysis of 
multistoried frames (No numerical problems). 
Matrix analysis of structures: 
Definition of flexibility and stiffness influence coefficients  
Flexibility method and Stiffness method: flexibility and stiffness 
matrices for truss and frame elements-load transformation matrix-
development of total flexibility and stiffness matrix of the structure-
analysis of simple structures-plane truss and plane frame-nodal loads 
and element loads-lack of fit and temperature effects. 
11 

 
 
4 
Structural dynamics: 
Introduction - degrees of freedom - equation of motion, D’Alembert’s 
principle-damping- free response of damped and undamped systems- 
logarithmic decrement-- single degree of freedom systems subjected to 
harmonic load - transient and steady state responses, simple portal 
frame problems.  
Moving loads and influence lines: Introduction to moving loads - 
concept of influence lines -influence lines for reaction, shear force and 
bending moment in simply supported beams and over hanging beams 
– analysis for single concentrated load, several concentrated loads, 
uniformly distributed load shorter and longer than the span – 
conditions for maximum bending moment and shear force.
