Course Objectives: 
1.  To understand the basic concepts and components of robotic systems. 
2.  To learn principles of robot kinematics, dynamics, and control. 
3.  To gain practical experience with robot programming and sensor integration. 
4.  To explore various applications of robotics in industry and research.

Fundamentals of Robotics :
Introduction to Robotics - Overview of robotics and its applications - 
Historical development and current trends - Basic components and 
classifications of robots 
Robot Anatomy and Components - Sensors: types, functions, and 
integration - Actuators: motors, servos, and their control - Control 
systems and feedback mechanisms 
Robot Kinematics - Coordinate systems and transformations - Forward 
and inverse kinematics 
Robot Dynamics - Introduction to dynamic modeling: Newton-Euler 
and Lagrangian methods

Control Systems and Programming : 
Control Systems Fundamentals - Control theory basics: PID control, 
state-space models - Implementing control algorithms in robotics 
Programming Robots - Introduction to robot programming languages 
and environments - Basic programming constructs and techniques 
Perception and Sensor Integration - Types of sensors: vision, 
rangefinders, IMUs - Sensor fusion and data interpretation 
9 
3 
Motion Planning and System Integration : 
Motion Planning - Path planning algorithms: A*, RRT, PRM - 
Motion planning in dynamic environments 
Robotic Systems Integration - Integration of hardware and software 
components - Communication protocols and data exchange 
Robotics in Industry - Applications in manufacturing, healthcare, and 
other fields - Case studies and industry guest speakers - Current 
trends and future directions in robotics 
9

Advanced Concepts in Robotics : 
Human-robot interaction: Techniques and applications - Autonomous 
robots: Navigation and decision-making - Robotics and AI: 
Integration and future trends 
Emerging Technologies - Soft robotics and bio-inspired robots 
Collaborative robots (cobots) and their applications - Swarm robotics
