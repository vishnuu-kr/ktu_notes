electronic devices.

Syllabus Description

Hours
1 
Wave shaping circuits: Sinusoidal and non-sinusoidal wave shapes, Principle 
and working of RC differentiating and integrating circuits, Clipping circuits - 
Positive, negative and biased clipper 
 
Transistor biasing: Introduction, operating point, concept of  
load line, fixed bias, self-bias and voltage divider bias. h-parameter model of 
BJT.  
 
11  
2 
Amplifiers: Classification of amplifiers, RC coupled amplifier –frequency 
response.  Analysis for mid frequency gain. Typical examples.  
. 
Power Amplifiers:  Definitions and classification. Typical circuits of Class 
A, Class B, Class AB and Class C Amplifiers. Analysis for conversion 
efficiency of B and AB. Simple examples.  
 
11  
3 
Operational amplifiers:  Block Diagram, ideal characteristics. Op-Amp 
Basics, Parameters like gain, bandwidth, slew rate, CMRR, offset voltage 
and offset current. IC741 op- amp characteristics. Simple op- amp circuits: 
Inverting amplifier, non-inverting amplifier, summing amplifier, integrator, 
difference amplifier.  
11  

 
Op-Amp Applications:  Instrumentation amplifier, Schmitt trigger, precision 
rectifiers, Comparators, zero crossing detector, level detector. Active Filters: 
Design of LPF, HPF, BPF and Notch filters. 
4 
Oscillators:  Feedback Concepts, Basic configurations, Effect of negative 
feedback on amplifiers. Oscillators:  
classification, criterion for oscillation, RC phase shift and Wien bridge 
oscillator (both using BJT). Design equations and working of the circuits 
(analysis not required). 
 
Regulated power supplies: Simple zener voltage regulator, series voltage 
regulator, IC 723  regulators: block diagram, typical low/high voltage 
circuits. 3 pin regulators: 78XX and 79XX 
 
11
