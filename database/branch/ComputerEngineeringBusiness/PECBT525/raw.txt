Hours 
1 
INTRODUCTION TO ECONOMICS– Concept of Economics and Business 
Economics, Micro Economics and Macro Economics, Fundamental concepts 
in economics that aid decision making: Incremental Principle, Concept of 
Cost, Discounting Principle, Time Concept, Concept of Inflation –Types and 
impact of inflation. Equi-Marginal Principle 
9 
2 
DEMAND ANALYSIS AND FORECASTING -- Demand Analysis – Types 
of Demand – Law of Demand & its Exceptions - Elasticity of Demand – Price 
Elasticity, Income Elasticity, Cross Elasticity, Advertisement Elasticity - 
Applications of the concept of Elasticity –- Demand Forecasting – An 
overview of Forecasting Techniques – Statistical & Non-Statistical Methods- 
Analysis of Consumer Equilibrium under Cardinal Utility Analysis. 
9 
3 
COST ANALYSIS & PRODUCTION ANALYSIS- Concepts of Production, 
production function with one variable input - Law of Variable Proportions. 
Production function with 2 variable inputs and Laws of returns to scale, 
Indifference Curves, ISO-Quants & ISO-Cost line, Least cost combination 
factor, Economies of scale, Diseconomies of scale. Technological progress 
and production function. Types of cost, Cost curves, Cost – Output 
Relationship in the short run and in the long run, LAC curve. Break Even 
9 

 
 
Analysis – Meaning, Assumptions, Determination of BEA, Limitations, Uses 
of BEA in Managerial decisions (with simple Problems). 
4 
MARKET STRUCTURES AND STRATEGIC INTERACTION: Overview 
of different market types: Perfect Competition, Monopoly, Monopolistic 
Competition, and Oligopoly-  In-depth analysis of profits in perfectly 
competitive and monopolistic markets.-Exploring sources of monopoly power 
and the concept of the multi-product firm- Investigating various models of 
oligopoly and understanding market failures.-Introduction to Game Theory for 
a strategic understanding of market interactions. 
9
