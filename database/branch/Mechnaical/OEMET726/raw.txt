Course Objectives: 
1. To familiarize students with basic understanding of fluid flow and heat transfer. 
2. To understand how to model simple heat transfer problems in one and two dimensions.

Hours 
1 
Basics of Heat Transfer-Conduction, convection and radiation heat 
transfer. 
Mathematical description of fluid flow and heat transfer: conservation 
equations for mass, momentum, energy and chemical species in Cartesian 
and cylindrical coordinates. Classification of Partial differential equations 
– elliptic, parabolic and hyperbolic equations. Initial and boundary 
conditions    
 
9 
2 
Finite difference form of PDE equations-Taylor’s series approach. Central 
difference, backward difference, and forward difference of first and second 
order derivatives. Solution of Laplace equation (conduction problems) 
using finite difference equations.  Discretization error, truncation error, 
round off error, Convergence of iteration. 
 
9 
3 
Solution of unsteady conduction equation using finite difference method- 
Explicit, implicit and semi-implicit methods. Stability of numerical 
solutions. Solution of linear algebraic equations- direct methods and 
indirect methods. Point by point iterations, Tri diagonal matrix method 
(TDMA) 
9 

 
4 
Introduction to Finite volume method. Solution of one-dimensional steady 
state conduction problems. Handling different boundary conditions. 
Solution of one-dimensional convection diffusion problems using central 
difference schemes and upwind scheme. Introduction to SIMPLE algorithm 
for incompressible flows.  
 
9
