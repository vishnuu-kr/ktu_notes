Course Objectives: 
1. To introduce the fundamentals and applications of op- -amps. 
2. To grasp the basics of digital electronics, including logic gates and Boolean algebra 
3. To design and implement combinational and sequential circuits to solve specific digital 
logic problems. 
4. To utilize breadboards or FPGAs or simulation software to implement circuits. 
5. To work in a group to design and implement a digital electronic system.

Introduction to Operational Amplifiers (Op-Amps) 
Op-amp Characteristics and ideal op-amp 
Op-amp configurations: inverting, non-inverting, differential amplifiers 
Op-amp parameters: gain, bandwidth, input/output impedance. 
Effect of feedback on op-amp performance 
Op-amp applications: amplifiers, comparators, integrators, differentiators, V-
I and I-V converters, Comparator – Regenerative comparator, zero crossing 
detector, Window detector, Sample and hold circuit, Rectifiers

2 
Active Filters and Signal Conditioning 
Low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, and band-stop filters using op-amps-Active 
filter design considerations 
Signal conditioning circuits for sensor interfacing using op-amps 
A-D and D-A converters using Op-amps 
D/A converter -specifications -weighted resistor type,R-2R Ladder type.  
A/D Converters – specifications-Flash type, Successive Approximation type, 
Dual Slope type 
11 
3 
Fundamentals of Digital Electronics 
Review of Boolean Algebra, Logic Gates- BCD, Gray code, Excess 3 code 
De-Morgan’s Theorems, Principle of Duality, Minimization of Boolean 
expressions, Sum of Products (SOP), Product of Sum (POS), Canonical 
forms, Karnaugh map Minimization.  Simplification of logic functions 
through K–maps and Implementation using logic gates (up to 4 variables). 
Combinational Logic Circuits 
Combinatorial Logic Systems - Comparators, Multiplexers, Demultiplexers, 
Encoder,Decoder. Half and Full Adders, Subtractors, Serial and Parallel 
Adders, BCD Adder. 
11 
4 
Sequential Logic Circuits 
Flip-flops and latches: SR, D, JK, and T flip-flops 
Design of sequential circuits-Counters-Ring counter and Johnsons counter; 
shift registers -SIPO, SISO, PISO, PIPO. 
Asynchronous Sequential Circuits- Analysis of state machines using D flip-
flops and JK flip flops; Design of state machines - state table, state 
assignment transition/excitation table
