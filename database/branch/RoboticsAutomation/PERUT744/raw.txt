Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the concept of analog communication. 
2. To analyse different digital communication schemes.

Hours 
1 
Analog Communication 
Block diagram of a communication system. Need for analog modulation. 
Amplitude modulation. Equation and spectrum of AM signal. DSB-SC and 
SSB systems. Block diagram of SSB transmitter and receiver. Frequency and 
phase modulation. Narrow and wide band FM and their spectra. FM 
transmitter and receiver. 
 
9  
2 
Stochastic processes and Source coding 
Review of probability. Bayes theorem and conditional probability. 
Stochastic processes, Stationarity. Conditions for WSS and SSS. 
Autocorrelation and power spectral density. LTI systems with WSS as 
input. Sampling and Quantization. Pulse code modulation, Transmitter and 
receiver. Companding. Practical 15 level A and mu-law companders. 
DPCM transmitter and receiver. Delta modulation. 
 
9  
3 
G-S Procedure and Effects in the Channel 
Definition of signal .Basic operation on signals. Gram-Schmitt procedure. 
Signal space. Baseband transmission through AWGN channel. Inter 
Symbol Interference. Nyquist criterion for zero ISI. Raised cosine and 
Square-root raised cosine spectrum, Partial response signalling and 
 9  

 
 
duobinary coding. Equalization. Vector model of AWGN channel. Matched 
filter and correlation receivers. MAP receiver, Maximum likelihood 
receiver and probability of error.  
4 
Digital Modulation Schemes 
Digital modulation schemes. Baseband BPSK system and the signal 
constellation. BPSK transmitter and receiver. Base band QPSK system and 
Signal constellations. Plots of BER Vs SNR with analysis. QPSK transmitter 
and receiver. Quadrature amplitude modulation and signal constellation.
