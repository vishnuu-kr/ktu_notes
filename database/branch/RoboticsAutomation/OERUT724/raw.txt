Course Objectives: 
1. Understand the principles and components of robotic systems used in assembly and material 
handling. 
2. Develop skills in programming and controlling robotic systems. 
3. Analyze the performance of robotic systems in industrial applications. 
4. Design and implement robotic solutions for material handling and assembly tasks

Introduction to Robotics and Industrial Automation - Introduction to 
Robotics- Definition and history of robotics - Types of robots and their 
applications - Overview of robotic systems in industrial automation. Robotic 
Components - Actuators, sensors, and controllers - End effectors and 
grippers - Robot kinematics and dynamics - Robotic Assembly Systems - 
Types of assembly robots - Applications in manufacturing - Advantages and 
limitations of robotic assembly

Robot Programming and Control - Basics of Robot Programming - 
Introduction to robot programming languages - Offline and online 
programming - Programming environments and simulation tools - Robot 
Control Systems - Open-loop and closed-loop control - PID control and 
advanced control strategies - Motion planning and trajectory generation - 
Sensor Integration and Feedback - Types of sensors used in robotics - Sensor 
fusion and data processing - Feedback control and real-time monitoring -

Industrial Robot Programming - Programming industrial robots (e.g., ABB, 
FANUC) - Common programming tasks and challenges - Case studies and 
practical examples. 
3 
Design and Analysis of Robotic Systems - Robotic System Design - Design 
principles and methodologies - Mechanical design and selection of 
components - Electrical and electronic considerations - Simulation and 
Modelling - Modelling robotic systems using CAD software - Kinematic and 
dynamic simulation - Validation and optimization of robotic designs - 
Performance Analysis - Metrics for evaluating robotic systems - Reliability, 
precision, and repeatability - Case studies on performance analysis - Safety 
and Standards - Safety considerations in robotic systems - International 
standards and regulations.

4 
Material Handling Systems - Concepts of material handling - principles 
and considerations in material handling systems design - conventional 
material handling systems Types of material handling robots – industrial 
trucks, monorails, rail guided vehicles, conveyor systems, cranes and hoists, 
advanced material handling systems, automated guided vehicle systems, 
automated storage and retrieval systems (ASRS), bar code technology, radio 
frequency identification technology. Applications and Future Trends - 
Advanced Robotic Applications - Collaborative robots (cobots) - Flexible 
manufacturing systems - Robotics in warehousing and logistics - AI and 
Machine Learning in Robotics - Introduction to AI and machine learning 
concepts - Applications in robotic vision and decision-making - Case studies 
on AI-driven robotic systems - Trends in robotic automation - Developments 
in robotic hardware and software - Potential future directions in robotic 
assembly and material handling
