Course Objectives: 
1. 
To understand the basic concepts of Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) systems. 
2. 
To become proficient in the design, development and implementation of IIoT systems  
 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Introduction to IoT and IIoT: IOT Definition, Importance of IoT, IoT 
architecture, Technology Behind IoT - Server End Technology, Sources of 
Internet of Things, M2M Communication. IoT Application Areas. 
The Various Industrial Revolutions, Role of Internet of Things (IoT) & 
Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) in Industry, Industry 4.0 revolutions, 
Support System for Industry 4.0. 
9 
2 
Implementation Systems for IIoT: Sensors and actuators for industrial 
processes, sensor networks: network topologies, types of networks. Study of 
different sensors and their characteristics: Light sensor, temperature sensor 
with thermistor, voltage sensor, ADC and DAC, Temperature and Humidity 
Sensor DHT11, Motion Detection Sensors, Wireless Bluetooth Sensors, 
Level Sensors. Actuators: Thermal, hydraulic, pneumatic, electromechanical. 
Communication Protocols: UART Communication Protocol, I2C Protocol 
device interfacing and decoding of signal, SPI Protocol device interfacing 
and decoding of signal. Application layer protocols: HTTP, CoAP, MQTT. 
9 

 
 
3 
Data Handling in IIoT: Role of microcontrollers in IIoT: Introduction to 
Raspberry Pi-Installation, programming, Interfaces (serial, SPI, I2C) 
Cloud Services:  Configuration of the cloud platform, Sending data from the 
IoT nodes to the gateways using different communication options; 
Transferring data from gateway to the cloud. Google, AWS, Microsoft Azure 
cloud architect. 
Case study: Program different Sensors for IoT applications 
Security and privacy issues in IoT 
9 
4 
Industrial IoT Applications:  Healthcare, Power Plants, Inventory 
Management & Quality Control, Plant Safety and Security (Including AR 
and VR safety applications), Facility Management. Oil, chemical and 
pharmaceutical industry, applications of UAVs in industries 
9
