Course Objectives: 
1. This course is designed to build a basic understanding on the various concepts involved in the 
design of different mechanisms of automated machines.

Introduction to Automated Systems 
Overview of Automated Systems: Structure of automatic industrial systems, 
Linear and circular configurations, Non-industrial robots 
Manufacturing Process Layouts: Processing layout, Case studies: Chain 
links, springs, soldering printed circuits, galvanizing steel strips, Approaches 
to automating manufacturing processes, Timing layout and productivity 
determination, 
9  
2 
Drive Systems and Dynamic Analysis  
Mechanical and Electromagnetic Drives: Mechanical drive systems: Falling 
weight, inclined belt, spring systems, Electromagnetic drives and electric 
drives: Induction motors, stepper motors 
Hydraulic and Pneumatic Drives: Hydraulic and pneumatic drives, Brakes 
and variable moment of inertia 
Dynamic Analysis: Calculation of selection parameters, Calculation of 
braking time for different systems 
9 
3 
Kinematics and Control  
Kinematics and Control Mechanisms: Position function and input-output 
relations, 4 slot Geneva mechanism, cam mechanisms, Amplifiers and 
flexible cam systems 
9  

 
 
Dynamic Accuracy and Damping: Dynamic accuracy and vibrations, 
Damping: Electrical and mechanical 
4 
Transportation, Feeding, and Functional Systems  
Transportation Devices: Linear and rotational transportation Conveyors, 
indexing tables, Case studies on transportation devices 
Feeding and Orientation Devices: Feeding systems: Liquid, powder, wire, 
rod 
Orientation: Passive, active, logical, and electromagnetic fields 
Functional Systems and Manipulators: Automatic assembling and inspection 
systems, Manipulators: Dynamics, kinematics, vibrations
