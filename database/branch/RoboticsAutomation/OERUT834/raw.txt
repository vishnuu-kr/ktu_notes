Course Objectives: 
1. To give students the ability to understand the concepts of image formation and processing 
2. To give students the ability to apply machine learning to various problems related to vision

Hours

Computer Vision – Definition, Difference between computer vison and 
machine vision, real world applications 
Image Formation:  Geometric primitives and transformations, 2D 
transformations, 3D transformations – Translation, Rotation + Translation, 
3D to 2D projections - Orthography and para-perspective, Perspective, 
Camera intrinsics, Camera matrix, Photometric image formation -  
Lighting,   The digital camera -  Image sensing pipeline,  factors affecting 
the performance of a digital image sensor 
 
9  
2 
Image Processing: Point operators - Pixel transforms,  Color transforms,   
Histogram equalization;  Linear filtering (Basic explanation only), Non 
Linear Filtering – Median Filtering; Binary Image Processing – 
Morphology, Distance Transforms, Connected Components  
 
9  
3 
Machine Learning: Traditional, machine learning, and deep learning 
pipelines; Supervised learning - Nearest neighbors, Bayesian classification; 
Unsupervised learning – Clustering, K-means and Gaussians mixture 
models; Deep neural networks - perceptron unit, Weights and layers, 
Activation functions, Loss functions, Backpropagation;  Convolutional 
9  

 
 
neural networks 
4 
Recognition:  Instance recognition, Image classification -   Feature-based 
methods, Deep networks, Object detection -  Face detection, General object 
detection -  Precision vs. recall, Semantic segmentation – Basic 
understanding only 
Feature Detection and Matching: Points and patches, Edge Detection, 
Contour Detection, Lines and vanishing points, Segmentation - Region 
splitting, Region merging, Watershed
