Course Type 
Theory 
 
Course Objectives: 
1. To make the students capable of analysing any linear time invariant electrical network. 
2. To study time domain, phasor and Laplace transform methods of linear circuit analysis. 
3. To study the transient response of networks subject to test signals. 
4. To develop understanding of the concept of  two port networks 
 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
 
 
 
1 
Mesh and Node Analysis: 
Introduction to circuit variables and circuit elements, Review of Kirchhoff’s 
Laws. Independent and dependent Sources, Source transformations. wye-delta 
transformation and delta-wye transformation. Mesh and node analysis of 
network containing independent and dependent sources. Super mesh and 
Super node analysis of network containing independent and dependent 
sources. 
 
 
 
9 
 
2 
Network Theorems (applied to both dc circuits having dependent source 
and independent source) 
Thevenin’s theorem, Norton’s theorem, 
Superposition theorem, Maximum 
power transfer theorem Reciprocity theorem, 
 
9 
 
 
3 
Application of Laplace Transforms: 
Review of Laplace Transforms Initial value theorem & Final value theorem 
(Proof not necessary) Transformation of basic signals and circuits into s- 
 
 
9 

 
 
domain. Transient analysis of RL, RC, and RLC networks with impulse, step, 
pulse, exponential and sinusoidal inputs . Analysis of networks with 
transformed impedance and dependent sources 
 
 
4 
Two port networks 
Impedance, Admittance, Transmission and Hybrid parameters of two port 
network. Interrelationship among parameter sets. Series and parallel 
connections of two port networks. Reciprocal and Symmetrical two port 
network. 
 
 
9
