Course Objectives: 
1. To impart the basic understanding of the theory and applications of database management 
systems in real-world scenarios. 
2. To enable students in designing, querying, and managing databases efficiently 
SYLLABUS 
Module 
No. 
Syllabus Description 
Contact 
Hours 
1 
Basics: Overview of Database Management Systems - Characteristics of 
database approach vs file system approach – Advantages & Applications – 
Three Schema Architecture - Three tier Architecture. 
Entity Relationship model:  Entity Types and Sets – Attributes and types, 
Keys - Relationships and types - Cardinality, Participation - Structural 
Constraints – Conventional and min-max Notations – Drawing and 
interpreting ER diagrams. 
Relational Model Concepts: Structure of Relational Databases - Domains, 
Attributes, Tuples, Relations – Types of Keys -Integrity Constraints - 
Synthesizing ER diagram to Relational Schema – Examples. 
 
9  
2 
Introduction to Relational Algebra: Select, Project, Rename, Union, 
Intersection, Difference, Division - Cartesian product – Join and its types – 
Relational algebra query examples. 
Structured Query Language (SQL): Data Types – Data Definition 
Language (DDL): Create, Alter, Drop, Truncate, rename – Data 
Manipulation Language (DML): Select, Insert, Update, Delete - Nested 
queries (correlated and non-correlated) - Aggregation and Grouping – 
9  

 
 
Views – Example SQL queries on single and multiple tables – Query 
Practise. 
3 
Normalization: DB Design Anomalies - Functional Dependencies - 
Armstrong’s Axioms – Closures - Equivalence of FDs, Minimal Cover - 
First Normal Form (1NF) - Second Normal Form (2NF) - Third Normal 
Form (3NF) - Boyce Codd Normal Form (BCNF) - Lossless Join 
Decomposition - Dependency Preserving Decomposition. 
Physical Data Organization: Physical and Logical records - Blocking 
Factor - Pinned and unpinned organization - Heap files – Sorted files – 
Indexing - Single-Level Ordered Indexes - Multilevel Indexes – Dynamic 
indexing – B Trees & B+ Trees. 
9  
4 
Transaction Processing Concepts: Transaction States and Operations – 
ACID Properties - Serial Schedules - Concurrent and Serializable 
Schedules - Conflict Equivalence and Conflict Serializability – 
Recoverable, Cascade-less & Strict Schedules - Locking - Two-phase 
locking and its variations - System Log – Log based recovery - Deferred 
database modification – Check pointing. 
9
