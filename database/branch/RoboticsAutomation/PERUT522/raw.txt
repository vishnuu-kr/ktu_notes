Course Objectives: 
1. To understand how design choices impact manufacturing costs and processes. 
2. To gain skills in tolerance analysis and understanding datum systems for precise 
manufacturing. 
3. To learn to minimize part count, standardize designs, and create assembly-friendly products.

Design Impact and Material Considerations - Design Impact on Cost: 
Understanding how design choices affect manufacturing costs. Design for 
‘X’ – DFM Approach: Overview of Design for Manufacturing principles and 
the DFM Framework. Material and Process Evaluation: Selecting 
appropriate materials and processes. General DFM Guidelines: Key 
guidelines for designing manufacturable products. Machining and Casting 
Guidelines and Examples: Specific considerations for machining and casting 
processes. Minimize Finishing Requirements: Strategies to reduce post-
processing needs. Facilitating Machining Design Features: Datum features, 
functional and manufacturing aspects. Component Design for Machining: 
Considerations and redesign examples. Form Design of Castings and 
Weldments: Design principles for castings and weldments. 
9 
2 
Tolerance Analysis and Selective Assembly - Tolerance Analysis: Process 
capability and metrics, cost aspects of tolerances. Feature and Geometric 
Tolerances: Understanding and applying tolerances in design. Relationship 
9 

 
 
between Tolerance Grades and Machining Processes: Attainable tolerance 
grades. Cumulative Effect of Tolerances: Sure fit law, normal law, and 
truncated normal law. Interchangeable Part Manufacture and Selective 
Assembly: Concepts and examples. Control of Axial Play: Techniques for 
controlling play, including secondary machining operations and laminated 
shims. 
3 
Datum Systems and True Position Theory - Datum Systems: Degrees of 
freedom, grouped datum systems, and their applications. Computational 
Accuracy: Translational and rotational accuracy calculations. Geometric 
Analysis: Applications of geometric analysis in design. True Position 
Theory: Understanding true position theory, including co-ordinate and 
conventional methods of feature location. Virtual Size Concept: Concepts of 
floating and fixed fasteners, projected tolerance zones. Assembly with 
Gasket and Zero True Position Tolerance: Practical applications and 
examples. Functional Gauges: Use and design of functional gauges. Paper 
Layout Gauging: Techniques and examples. 
9 
4 
Principles of Design for Assembly - Minimize Part Count: Strategies to 
reduce the number of parts in a design. Standardization and Minimize Part 
Variety: Principles of standardization and variety reduction. Design 
Guidelines for Manual Assembly: Best practices for designing for manual 
assembly. DFA Analysis: Conducting DFA analysis and calculating DFA 
index. Design for Automated Assembly: Principles and guidelines for 
automated assembly. Introduction to DFMA Software: Overview of software 
tools used for DFMA, including practical applications and case studies.
