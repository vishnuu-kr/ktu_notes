Course Objectives: 
1. To understand the fundamentals of network theorems and apply these concepts for steady state 
analysis of circuits 
2. To apply the concept of Laplace transform for studying the transient response of RLC circuits 
3. To analyze the two port networks using network functions and parameters

Hours 
1 
Network Theorems 
Review of mesh and node analysis, Super mesh and Super node Analysis – 
Analysis of DC and AC circuits containing independent and dependent 
sources, Network Theorems - Thevenin’s theorem, Norton’s theorem, 
Superposition theorem, Reciprocity theorem, Maximum power transfer 
theorem.  Steady state AC analysis – Application of network theorems to 
solve AC circuits.   
11 
2 
Circuit Analysis through Laplace transform 
Review of Laplace transform and inverse Laplace transform, Initial value 
theorem and Final value theorem (no proof required) - Transformation of 
circuits into s-domain, Transient analysis of RL, RC and RLC circuits 
through Laplace transform with impulse, step and sinusoidal inputs (with 
11 

 
 
and without initial conditions), Analysis of networks with transformed 
impedance and dependent sources. 
3 
Network Functions – Poles & Zeros 
Network functions of single port and two port networks – Driving point 
impedance and transfer functions, Computation of network functions for 
Ladder and non-Ladder networks, Poles and zeros of network functions – 
Significance of poles and zeros, Properties of driving point and transfer 
functions, Necessary conditions for driving point and transfer functions, 
Time domain response from pole-zero plot, Amplitude and phase response 
from pole-zero plot, Stability Criteria. 
11 
4 
Two Port Network Parameters  
Two port networks - Impedance, admittance, hybrid and transmission 
parameters – conditions for reciprocity and symmetry, Inter relationships of 
different parameters, Interconnection of two port networks - Series, & 
Parallel.  T and π equivalent of a two-port network, Image impedance 
parameter description of a two-port network, Characteristic impedance and 
propagation constant of a symmetrical two port network (derivation not 
required). 
11
